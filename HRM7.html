💵 WAGES vs SALARY & PACKAGE COMPONENTS
Fundamentals of Compensation
COMP
WAGES vs SALARY
Wages Compensation for manual/hourly workers; Paid on hourly/daily/weekly basis; Variable with hours worked; Lower skill roles
Salary Compensation for salaried/white-collar employees; Paid monthly/annually; Fixed; Higher skill roles; Professional work
Key Difference Wages = pay for TIME worked (hourly); Salary = pay for JOB/ROLE (fixed regardless of hours); Wages variable; Salary fixed
Legal Implication Code on Wages 2020: Applies primarily to wage workers; Minimum wage, overtime, gratuity provisions; Different from salaried staff
WAGE COMPONENTS
Basic Wage Core compensation; Hourly or daily rate; Basis for all calculations
Dearness Allowance (DA) Adjustment for inflation; Percentage of basic; Indexed to CPI; Regular updates
House Rent Allowance (HRA) Location-based; Percentage of basic; Varies by city tier
Other Allowances Travel, medical, conveyance, special allowances; Job-specific add-ons
Bonus/Incentives Performance-based; Year-end bonus; Variable component; Linked to targets
SALARY PACKAGE COMPONENTS
Fixed Components Basic salary, allowances, HRA, DA; Guaranteed monthly
Variable Components Performance bonus, commission, incentives; Depends on performance/results
Benefits Health insurance, life insurance, pension, gratuity, leave encashment
Perquisites Car allowance, club membership, housing, education allowance; Senior management
FACTORS INFLUENCING (PYQ 2025)
Internal Factors Job evaluation, pay grades, performance, seniority, skills, qualifications
External Factors Market rates, industry standards, cost of living, inflation, labor supply, competition
Organizational Budget constraints, profitability, strategic priorities, union agreements
WAGES vs SALARY DISTINCTION
Wages: Manual workers, hourly paid, variable with hours, governed by Code on Wages
Salary: Salaried employees, monthly fixed, professional roles, broader benefits
Legal Framework: Different provisions apply; Different compliance requirements

Principles (PYQ 2016) & Incentive Schemes (PYQ 2013)
ADMIN
PRINCIPLES OF WAGE ADMIN (PYQ 2016)
Job Evaluation "We pay for jobs, not men" — Compensation based on job content not person; Fair value for role
Equity & Fairness Equal pay for equal work; Internal equity between roles; External equity with market
Transparency Clear communication of pay scales; Criteria explained; No hidden discrimination; Trust building
Legal Compliance Minimum wage adherence; Code on Wages 2020 compliance; Equal Remuneration Act compliance; No discrimination
Market Competitiveness Competitive with market rates; Attract and retain talent; Benchmark with industry standards
INDIVIDUAL INCENTIVES (PYQ 2013)
Piece-Rate System Pay per unit produced; Direct link to output; High performers earn more; Motivates productivity
Commission Percentage of sales; Sales roles; Direct link to revenue; Common in retail, insurance
Performance Bonus Based on individual performance metrics; KPIs achieved; Year-end bonus structure
Merit Increase Base salary increase for performance; Annual review-based; Cumulative effect over time
Advantage Motivates high performers; Links pay to results; Rewards excellence; Can demotivate average performers
GROUP INCENTIVES (PYQ 2013)
Profit Sharing Share organization profits with employees; Collective benefit; Aligns interests; Annual distribution
Gainsharing Share productivity/efficiency gains with team; Team-wide incentive; Requires collective effort
Team Bonus Bonus for team achieving targets; Encourages collaboration; Interdependent goals
Advantage Promotes teamwork; Reduces internal competition; Aligns organization and employee interests; Collective responsibility
INCENTIVE DESIGN FACTORS
Clarity Clear metrics; Objective measurement; Transparent calculation; Everyone understands
Achievability Realistic targets; Motivational but achievable; Not discouraging; Link to performance
Fairness Equitable measurement; Free from bias; Review for discrimination; Trust in system
"WE PAY FOR JOBS, NOT MEN" (PYQ 2022)
Meaning: Job evaluation determines pay; Not personal characteristics
Implication: Prevents bias; Ensures fairness; People in same job get similar pay
Application: Eliminates gender, age, caste-based discrimination in pay

🎁 CAFETERIA BENEFITS & MOTIVATION THEORIES
Flexible Benefits (PYQ 2014) & Maslow/Herzberg (PYQ 2018)
BENEFITS
CAFETERIA BENEFITS (PYQ 2014)
Definition Flexible benefits system where employees choose from menu of benefits; Customize package to individual needs
How It Works Core benefits provided to all; Flexible pool for choices; Employee selects based on preference; Budget allocation per person
Benefits Offered Health insurance, pension, life insurance, education, housing allowance, transportation, childcare
Advantage Meets diverse needs (single vs family, young vs senior); Personal relevance increases satisfaction; Better ROI on benefits
Challenge Administrative complexity; Requires IT system; Employee education needed; Adverse selection risks
MASLOW HIERARCHY (PYQ 2018)
Level 1: Physiological Food, shelter, safety; Base salary meets this; Without this, motivation impossible
Level 2: Safety Job security, benefits, predictability; Health insurance, retirement, stable income
Level 3: Social Belonging, relationships, team; Work environment, team dynamics, social events
Level 4: Esteem Recognition, respect, status; Titles, recognition, challenging work, responsibility
Level 5: Self-Actualization Growth, development, potential realization; Career development, learning, meaningful work
HERZBERG TWO-FACTOR (PYQ 2018)
Hygiene Factors Absence doesn't motivate but presence dissatisfies; Salary, benefits, working conditions, policies, security
Motivators Actual motivation; Challenging work, recognition, growth, responsibility, achievement
Key Insight Pay/benefits NECESSARY but NOT sufficient for motivation; Must add meaningful work, recognition, growth
Implication High pay without development = demotivated; Need BOTH good compensation AND meaningful work
COMPENSATION STRATEGY
Maslow Application Base compensation covers Level 1-2; Benefits for security; Development for self-actualization
Herzberg Application Fix hygiene factors first (compensation, conditions); Then add motivators (growth, recognition, challenge)
Balanced Approach Competitive pay + benefits (table stakes) + meaningful work + growth (competitive advantage)
MASLOW vs HERZBERG FOR COMPENSATION
Maslow: Hierarchical needs; Pay satisfies lower levels; Development satisfies higher levels
Herzberg: Pay is hygiene factor; Doesn't motivate but absence dissatisfies; Need motivators for real engagement
Practical: Combine approaches - competitive pay + meaningful work = engaged workforce

🛡️ EMPLOYEE WELFARE & SIGNIFICANCE
Types of Welfare Measures & Impact
WELFARE
TYPES OF WELFARE
Statutory Welfare Legally mandated; Minimum wage, leave, safety, working hours; Government-regulated compliance
Occupational Welfare Provided at workplace; Medical facilities, safety equipment, canteen, rest areas
Extra-Mural Welfare Outside workplace; Housing, education, recreation facilities; Extends care to family
Personal Welfare Individual focused; Counselling, training, personal development, career guidance
SIGNIFICANCE 1-2
Improves Morale & Reduces Grievances Employees feel valued beyond wages; Sense of being cared for; Reduces complaints; Improves loyalty
Reduces Absenteeism & Turnover Basic needs met at/near workplace; Less reason to seek alternatives; Better job satisfaction; Lower attrition
SIGNIFICANCE 3-4
Increases Labour Productivity Healthy, secure workers perform better; Maslow levels 1-2 addressed; Focus on work not survival; Output increases
CSR & Employer Brand Reflects organizational ethics; Enhances ESG ratings; Improves employer brand; Attracts talent; Stakeholder trust
SIGNIFICANCE 5-6
Industrial Harmony Welfare reduces conflict, fosters cooperation; Workers less likely to join radical unions; Better IR climate; Fewer strikes
Social Stability Welfare beyond workers (family) reduces societal pressure; Community development; Sustainable business model
WELFARE IMPACT CHAIN
Welfare Provisions → Meeting Basic Needs → Improved Morale → Higher Motivation → Better Performance
Secondary Effects: Lower turnover, fewer grievances, industrial harmony, CSR benefits

📊 ABSENTEEISM: CAUSES, IMPACT & CONTROL
Challenge & Solutions (PYQ 2015, 2025)
ABSENCE
CAUSES OF ABSENTEEISM
Personal/Health Illness, accidents, medical appointments, personal emergencies, family responsibilities
Work-Related Job dissatisfaction, poor management, conflict, lack of recognition, unsafe conditions
Social/External Transportation issues, social events, cultural obligations, weather conditions
Psychological Burnout, stress, depression, anxiety, lack of motivation, feeling undervalued
IMPACT OF ABSENTEEISM
Productivity Loss Reduced output; Deadlines missed; Team workload imbalance; Efficiency drops
Cost Impact Overtime payment; Replacement costs; Lost revenue; Equipment idle; Replacement worker inefficiency
Team Morale Others cover work; Frustration builds; Resentment toward absent colleague; Team conflict
Customer Impact Service delays; Quality issues; Customer dissatisfaction; Reputation damage
CONTROL MEASURES
Prevention Good working conditions; Fair management; Recognition; Development opportunities; Welfare measures
Monitoring Track absence patterns; Identify trends; Notice serial absentees; Distinguish legitimate from habitual
Policies Clear attendance expectations; Fair leave policy; Attendance monitoring; Progressive discipline for habitual
Support Employee assistance programs; Counselling; Medical support; Flexible working; Address underlying causes
Incentives Reward good attendance; Bonus for zero absence; Recognition; Privilege for reliable attendance
MANAGEMENT APPROACH
Fair but Firm Support legitimate absences; Discipline habitual absences; Distinguish avoidable from unavoidable
Root Cause Analysis Don't just punish; Understand WHY absent; Address underlying issues; Preventive approach
Holistic Strategy Prevention + Monitoring + Support + Incentive + Discipline; Not just discipline; Multi-faceted
ABSENTEEISM AS INDICATOR
Signal: High absenteeism signals workplace problems - dissatisfaction, poor management, work stress
Action: Don't just discipline; Investigate root causes; Address organizational issues
Prevention: Good workplace culture, recognition, support → naturally reduces absenteeism

⬆️ PROMOTIONS, TRANSFERS & SENIORITY vs MERIT
Career Progression Mechanisms (PYQ 2016)
MOBILITY
SENIORITY BASIS
Definition Promotion based on length of service; Time in role; Tenure in organization; "First in, first promoted"
Advantage Objective, measurable; No bias; Fair for long-serving employees; Reward loyalty; Predictable
Disadvantage May promote unqualified; Demotivates talented juniors; No performance link; Can embed mediocrity
Use In Government, PSUs, union-led organizations; Historically India; Traditional IR approach
MERIT BASIS
Definition Promotion based on capability, performance, qualifications; "Best person for role"; Competitive
Advantage Rewards performance; Motivates excellence; Promotes capable people; Improves organizational performance; Fairer long-term
Disadvantage Perceptions of bias possible; Requires fair evaluation systems; Can demotivate seniors passed over; Requires transparency
Use In Private sector, MNCs, modern organizations; Increasingly in Indian companies; Progressive IR approach
TRANSFERS (PYQ 2016)
Definition Lateral movement between positions; Same level; Different dept/location; No promotion or demotion
Types Production transfer (staffing needs), Penal (disciplinary), Promotional (broadens experience), Personal (family reasons)
Purposes Meet staffing requirements, Develop well-rounded managers, Prevent stagnation, Handle conflicts, Employee development
Impact Broadens exposure, Prevents boredom, Develops skills, Can be disruptive if not managed well, Geographic mobility required
COMBINED APPROACH
Best Practice Combine seniority + merit; Seniority as tiebreaker; Qualified seniors preferred; Ensures fairness AND performance
Modern Trend Move from pure seniority to merit-based with seniority safeguards; Performance management systems; Transparent criteria
India Context PSUs shifting from seniority to merit; Government sector more rigid; Private sector fully merit-based
SENIORITY vs MERIT DEBATE (PYQ 2016)
Seniority: Fair to long-serving, objective, traditional, but can embed mediocrity
Merit: Rewards performance, improves organization, but needs fair systems to avoid bias
Solution: Combined approach balances fairness to loyalty with need for capability

🚪 SEPARATIONS, EXIT POLICY & ATTRITION
Types, Exit Policy Features, IT Sector Challenge (PYQ 2016, 2014)
SEPARATION
TYPES OF SEPARATIONS
Resignation Employee initiates; Voluntary departure; Gives notice; Personal choice
Retirement Reaches retirement age; End of service; Gratuity and pension payable
Termination Organization initiates; For-cause; Poor performance, misconduct, violation; Notice period varies
Retrenchment Organization-initiated; Economic reasons; Surplus staff; Statutory compensation required
VRS (Voluntary Retirement Scheme) Voluntary; Enhanced benefits offered; Employee chooses; Usually in downsizing situations
BALANCED EXIT POLICY (PYQ 2016)
Transparent Severance Clearly defined compensation; No ambiguity; Fair across similar roles; Communicated upfront
Adequate Notice Period Based on grade and tenure; Respect for dignity; Time for employee to plan; Usually 1-3 months
Fair Benefit Calculation PF, gratuity, leave encashment, ESOP vesting correctly calculated; Third-party verification; No deductions
Non-Discrimination No bias based on gender, religion, caste; Constitution compliance; Article 16 adherence; Fair treatment
Grievance Mechanism Accessible appeal channel; Can dispute separation decision; Independent review; Fair hearing
EXIT POLICY FEATURES 2
Outplacement Services Job search assistance; Skill retraining; Resume help; Interview coaching; Social responsibility
Legal Compliance Industrial Disputes Act compliance; Code on IR 2020 requirements; Chapter V-B provisions; Avoid litigation
Humane Treatment Exit interviews; Proper farewell; References provided; Professional closure; Affects remaining employees' morale
Documentation Record exit reasons; Clear documentation; Handle disputes; Audit trail; Protect organization
IT SECTOR ATTRITION (PYQ 2014)
Challenge High voluntary attrition; Rapid job hopping; Talent poaching; Skill shortages; Retention difficult
Causes Better external opportunities; Global mobility; Stock option differences; Project-based stress; Limited growth visibility
Solutions Competitive compensation, ESOP schemes, clear career paths, meaningful projects, work-life balance, retention bonuses
Impact Loss of IP, client relationships, project continuity; High replacement costs; Knowledge gaps; Team instability
BALANCED EXIT POLICY IMPORTANCE (PYQ 2016)
Fair Treatment: Protects employee dignity; Ensures legal compliance; Prevents disputes
Organizational Impact: Affects remaining employee morale; Reputation impacts recruitment; Ethical signal
Strategic: Even exit is opportunity to manage employment brand and maintain goodwill

IR Challenges (PYQ 2016) & PYQ Mapping
PSU IR
DISINVESTMENT CHALLENGES (PYQ 2016)
Job Insecurity Anxiety Uncertainty about service conditions; Worry about pay parity with private acquirers; Loss of government job security guarantees
Union Opposition BSNL, Air India, BHEL unions historically resisted; Strikes and work-to-rule actions; INTUC and BMS opposed; Collective bargaining issues
VRS Dilemma BSNL VRS 2019 (78,000 takers); Air India pre-privatisation VRS; Mixed results; Voluntary take-up varies by age and alternatives
Service Condition Impact Pension changes; Healthcare reduction; Leave policies modified; Job security lost; Psychological impact
EMPLOYEE INTEREST PROTECTION
Pre-Announcement Consultation Involve trade unions from outset; Transparent communication; Address concerns early; Build buy-in
Service Condition Protection Clauses in sale agreement; Minimum 2-3 years job guarantee; Protect existing staff; Binding on acquirer
Generous VRS Package Above-statutory compensation; Counselling support; Coaching for those opting out; Financial security for exit
Redeployment Options Transfer to other PSUs; Skill retraining through PMKVY; Alternate employment pathways; Keep within ecosystem
Employee Shareholding Shares offered at preferential rates; Builds ownership mindset; Financial upside potential; Aligns interests
FURTHER PROTECTIONS
Regulatory Safeguards SEBI, Competition Commission oversight; Acquirer employment promises monitored; Fair dealing requirements
Legal Framework Industrial Disputes Act provisions; Code on IR 2020 requirements; Chapter V-B compliance; Statutory protection
Grievance Mechanisms Appeal channels; Independent review; Labor court access; Dispute resolution; Fair hearing assured
