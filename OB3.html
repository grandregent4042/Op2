MOTIVATION IN ORGANISATIONAL BEHAVIOUR

Understanding What Drives People & How to Sustain Performance

Concepts & Characteristics

⚡ DEFINITION & NATURE
MOTIVATION
Core Definition
Internal Force
The psychological energy, drive, or willingness that propels individuals toward action. Arises from within, not externally imposed
Goal-Directed Behaviour
Motivation is purpose-driven. Individuals exert effort toward specific objectives that have personal value and meaning
Dynamic & Continuous
Not static; motivation fluctuates based on changing needs, circumstances, and satisfaction levels. Ongoing process throughout career
Intrinsic vs Extrinsic
Intrinsic: Motivation from within (autonomy, mastery, purpose)
Extrinsic: External rewards (money, praise, recognition)
The Motivation Cycle
Need → Drive → Action → Satisfaction → Rest until New Need Emerges
A person identifies unmet need → develops drive to fulfill it → takes action toward goal → achieves satisfaction → cycle may restart with new need

Content Theories: What Motivates?

🏔️ MASLOW'S HIERARCHY OF NEEDS
FIVE LEVEL PYRAMID
The Five Levels (Bottom to Top)
Physiological Needs
Food, water, shelter, sleep, safety. Basic survival needs must be met first. Salary, work environment conditions satisfy these
Safety Needs
Security, stability, freedom from fear. Job security, predictable work, health insurance address these in organization
Social & Belonging
Need for connection, acceptance, relationships. Team belonging, camaraderie, mentorship fulfill these needs
Esteem Needs
Recognition, respect, achievement, status. Promotions, awards, meaningful work, leadership opportunities satisfy these
Self-Actualization
Realizing full potential, personal growth, meaningful contribution. Learning opportunities, challenging assignments, legacy-building work
Key Principle: Prepotency
Lower needs must be satisfied before higher needs motivate: Hungry person won't care about recognition. Only unsatisfied needs motivate. Satisfied needs no longer drive behavior
🎯 HERZBERG'S TWO-FACTOR THEORY
Two Distinct Dimensions
Hygiene Factors
Prevent Dissatisfaction (Not Create Satisfaction)
Salary, working conditions, company policy, supervision. Their absence causes dissatisfaction; presence only removes dissatisfaction
Motivators
Create Satisfaction & Motivation
Achievement, recognition, meaningful work, responsibility, growth, advancement. These actually motivate people
Dual Continuum Concept
Satisfaction and dissatisfaction are NOT opposites on same scale. They're separate dimensions. Need both good hygiene AND motivators
Job Enrichment
Adding motivators to work through greater responsibility, autonomy, skill variety, task significance, and feedback loops
🏆 McCLELLAND'S ACQUIRED NEEDS THEORY
Three Learned Needs
Need for Achievement (nAch)
Desire to excel, accomplish challenging goals, succeed. High nAch people: entrepreneurs, high performers, goal-focused. Respond to challenging tasks
Need for Affiliation (nAff)
Desire for belonging, friendly relationships, cooperation. High nAff people: team players, counselors, HR professionals. Prefer collaboration over competition
Need for Power (nPow)
Desire to influence, control, lead others. High nPow people: leaders, entrepreneurs, politicians. Seek impact and authority positions
Learned Through Culture
These needs are acquired through socialization, not innate. Can be developed through training and experience. Cultural context shapes which needs dominate
🔄 ALDERFER'S ERG THEORY
Three Collapsed Need Levels
Existence Needs
Basic needs (physiological + safety): Food, shelter, safety, security. Similar to Maslow's lower two levels
Relatedness Needs
Desire for relationships, belonging, community. Similar to Maslow's social and esteem needs (interpersonal aspects)
Growth Needs
Self-actualization, personal development, challenge, achievement. Desire to become all you can be
Frustration-Regression
If growth needs frustrated, person regresses to seeking relatedness satisfaction. If relatedness frustrated, regresses to existence. Explains apparent backward movements

Process Theories: How Motivation Works?

🎲 VROOM'S EXPECTANCY THEORY
MOTIVATION FORMULA
Three Key Components
Expectancy (E)
Person's belief that effort WILL lead to performance. Do I believe if I work hard, I'll perform well? Range: 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain)
Instrumentality (I)
Person's belief that performance WILL lead to outcomes/rewards. Do I believe good performance will be rewarded? Range: 0 to 1
Valence (V)
Value/importance person places on reward. How much do I actually want that reward? Range: -1 (negative) to +1 (highly valued)
The Formula
MOTIVATION = EXPECTANCY × INSTRUMENTALITY × VALENCE
If ANY component is zero, motivation is zero. All three must be positive for motivation to occur
Managerial Implication
Strengthen all three: (1) Build confidence that effort leads to performance (training, clear job roles), (2) Ensure performance gets rewarded (transparent reward systems, merit-based), (3) Offer rewards people value (varied reward choices)
⚖️ ADAMS' EQUITY THEORY
Fairness Drives Motivation
Input-Output Ratio
People compare their inputs (effort, education, experience) to outputs (pay, recognition, benefits). Motivation depends on perceived fairness of this ratio
Social Comparison
Not absolute fairness that matters, but fairness relative to referents (similar others). Compare your ratio to colleague's ratio
Underreward vs Overreward
Underreward: Feel cheated, reduce effort, seek higher pay
Overreward: Feel guilty, increase effort (temporary). Psychological discomfort
🎯 PORTER-LAWLER & GOAL SETTING
Extended Models
Porter-Lawler Model
Extension of expectancy theory. Adds role clarity and ability. Satisfaction follows performance (not drives it). Performance depends on effort, ability, and role clarity
Locke's Goal Setting
Specific, difficult (but achievable) goals increase motivation and performance more than "do your best" goals. Clear targets focus effort
SMART Framework
Specific Measurable Achievable Relevant Time-bound
Feedback & Commitment
Goals must have feedback mechanisms (know progress) and public commitment increases motivation. Regular progress reviews sustain motivation

Indian Context & Contemporary Applications

🏛️ PUBLIC SECTOR CHALLENGES (INDIA)
Motivation Gaps in Government
Weak Instrumentality in APAR
Annual Performance Appraisal & Reporting: Historically weak link between performance and rewards. Everyone gets similar ratings/increments regardless of effort. Kills expectancy and instrumentality
Mission Karmayogi Reform
Government's new competency-based appraisal system. Aims to strengthen performance-reward linkage, increase merit-based promotions, improve instrumentality in public sector motivation
Seniority-Based Promotions
Traditional seniority over merit reduces motivation for high performers. If promotion guaranteed by time, not effort, high achievers (nAch) lose motivation. Causes brain drain
🚀 CONTEMPORARY MOTIVATIONAL TRENDS
Emerging Patterns
Gig Economy Hygiene Deficit
Gig workers (Ola, Uber, Zomato) lack basic hygiene factors: job security, benefits, stable income. Creates dissatisfaction even with high earning potential. Threatens retention
Startup nAch Culture
Startups attract high-achievement individuals with: challenging goals, equity stakes, growth opportunity. Intrinsic motivation through autonomy, mastery, purpose drives startup employees
Remote Work Motivations
Post-COVID shift: Flexibility, autonomy, reduced commute fulfill esteem and growth needs. But relatedness needs challenge (isolation). Hybrid models balance both
🇮🇳 CULTURAL FACTORS IN INDIAN MOTIVATION
Hofstede Dimensions Impact
High Power Distance
Indian organizations accept hierarchical authority. Manager approval, status symbols, position matter greatly. Recognition from authority figures highly valued as motivator
Collectivist Social Needs
Group harmony and belonging more important than individual achievement. Team recognition, family benefits, job security motivate more than high individual compensation
Relationship-Based Trust
Loyalty to manager and organization matters. Personal connection and mentor-mentee relationships are motivation drivers alongside formal rewards

Integrated Framework & Action Plan

🔗 COMPLEMENTARY NATURE OF THEORIES
How Theories Work Together
Content Theories: WHAT
Maslow, Herzberg, McClelland, Alderfer answer "What are human needs?" Provide insights on what drives people. Foundation for understanding individual differences
Process Theories: HOW
Vroom, Adams, Goal Setting answer "How does motivation work?" Explain the mechanism of how rewards lead to motivation. Practical application focus
Synthesis in Practice
Use content theories to identify what your employees need (achievement, belonging, growth). Use process theories to design systems that ensure effort→performance→reward linkage works
📋 SEVEN-STEP MANAGERIAL ACTION PLAN
Implementing Motivation in Organizations
1. Diagnose Needs: Assess what motivates your team (surveys, one-on-ones). Don't assume; understand individual differences
2. Clarify Role: Make job roles and expectations crystal clear. Removes uncertainty, builds expectancy that effort leads to performance
3. Set Goals: Use SMART goals. Challenging but achievable. Provides direction and measurement for performance
4. Remove Hygiene Issues: Ensure basic salary, conditions, security are adequate. Don't expect motivation if basics are missing
5. Provide Motivators: Build in autonomy, meaningful work, growth opportunities, recognition. These actually inspire discretionary effort
6. Link Performance to Rewards: Make clear that good performance leads to valued rewards. Strengthen instrumentality through transparent systems
7. Give Feedback & Reinforce: Regular feedback on progress. Reinforce desired behaviors. Celebration of wins sustains long-term motivation
🔁 REINFORCEMENT THEORY (SKINNER)
Sustaining Motivation Over Time
Purpose: Sustainability
Content and process theories explain initial motivation. Reinforcement ensures it's sustained. Consistent feedback, variable rewards, positive reinforcement keep motivation alive long-term
Positive Reinforcement
Reward desired behavior immediately after it occurs. Increases likelihood of repetition. "Great presentation!" right after performance
Schedules of Reinforcement
Fixed Ratio Variable Ratio Fixed Interval Variable Interval
Avoid Punishment Focus
Punishment (negative consequences) suppresses behavior but creates fear, resentment. Positive reinforcement builds intrinsic motivation and healthy culture
