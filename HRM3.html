📋 RECRUITMENT FUNDAMENTALS - Definitions & Objectives
Flippo | Yoder | Nature & Characteristics | 4 Objectives
RECRUITMENT
DEFINITIONS (Memorise Flippo)
Edwin Flippo (MOST COMPREHENSIVE) "Process of searching for prospective employees and stimulating them to apply for jobs in the organisation"
Dale Yoder "Process to discover sources of manpower to meet staffing schedule requirements and employ effective measures for attracting adequate manpower"
Gary Dessler "Activities necessary to attract qualified candidates to apply for job positions"
KEY DISTINCTION (EXAM CRITICAL) Recruitment = POSITIVE process (enlarges pool) | Selection = NEGATIVE process (eliminates from pool)
CHARACTERISTICS & NATURE
Positive process Enlarges candidate pool (unlike selection which eliminates)
Linking activity Connects job-givers and job-seekers in labour market
Continuous process Ongoing in large, growing organisations with regular vacancies
Two-way process Both organisation and candidate evaluate each other
Precedes selection Provides raw material for selection decisions
OBJECTIVES (4 KEY OBJECTIVES)
1. Attract sufficient qualified pool At minimum cost; right candidates at right time
2. Meet legal & social obligations Reservations (SC/ST/OBC/PwD), EEO compliance, diversity targets
3. Increase organisational effectiveness Short-term and long-term performance and capability
4. Create talent pipeline Reduces future hiring urgency; prepares for growth; builds employer brand

⚙️ RECRUITMENT PROCESS - 7-STEP MODEL
From Vacancy Identification to Candidate Communication
7 STEPS
STEP 1-2: Vacancy & Job Analysis
STEP 1: Identify Vacancies Triggered by HRP; determine number, type, level of vacancies; budget approval; requisition created
STEP 2: Job Analysis Job Description (duties, conditions) + Job Specification (qualifications, skills); define role clearly
STEP 3-4: Sourcing & Applications
STEP 3: Source Candidates Choose internal/external sources; activate recruitment channels; decide on media
STEP 4: Generate Applications Advertise | Campus drives | Agency referrals | E-recruitment portals | Employee referrals | Walk-ins
STEP 5-7: Screen, Evaluate, Control
STEP 5: Screen Applications Shortlist candidates meeting minimum criteria; reject non-qualifying; create short-list (typically 10-20 from 100+)
STEP 6: Communicate to Candidates Call letters | Interview invitations | Email notifications | Status updates
STEP 7: Evaluate & Control Analyse cost-per-hire | Time-to-fill | Quality-of-hire metrics | Improve process efficiency

🔄 INTERNAL SOURCES - 5 TYPES
Promotions | Transfers | Referrals | Job Posting | Re-employment
INTERNAL SOURCES
5 INTERNAL SOURCE TYPES
1. Promotions & Transfers Upward or lateral movement of existing employees within organisation
2. Employee Referrals Existing employees recommend candidates from their networks
3. Job Posting / Bidding Vacancies advertised internally on notice boards or intranet; employees apply
4. Re-employment of Former Employees "Boomerang hires" - retired or resigned talent rejoining organisation
5. Succession Plans / Talent Database HR identifies ready talent from bench strength and succession pipelines
MERITS OF INTERNAL
✓ Motivating Creates sense of career growth for existing employees
✓ Cost-effective Saves advertising & agency fees; lower onboarding costs
✓ Performance known Track record available; reduces risk of mismatch
✓ Faster process No extensive orientation needed; knows systems and culture
✓ Continuity Minimises disruption to operations and team dynamics
LIMITATIONS OF INTERNAL
✗ Limited pool Restricted to existing workforce; may lack specific skills
✗ Inbreeding risk Limited external perspectives; perpetuates existing culture
✗ Resentment May demoralise non-promoted employees; fairness concerns
✗ Vacancy cascade Creates another vacancy when someone promoted or transferred
✗ Blocks innovation No fresh ideas from outside; may block organisational change

🌍 EXTERNAL SOURCES - 7 TYPES
Advertisement | Agencies | Campus | Walk-ins | Exchanges | Professional Bodies | E-Recruitment
EXTERNAL SOURCES
7 EXTERNAL SOURCE TYPES
1. Direct Recruitment / Walk-ins For unskilled/semi-skilled workers; factory gates; spontaneous applications
2. Advertisements Newspapers | Job portals (Naukri, LinkedIn, Monster) | Company website | Social media
3. Employment Exchanges Statutory under Employment Exchanges Act, 1959; compulsory notification of vacancies (govt)
4. Recruitment/Placement Agencies Specialised/senior roles; headhunting (Korn Ferry, EMA Partners); executive search firms
5. Campus Recruitment Fresher hiring from colleges, IIMs, IITs, professional institutes; bulk hiring; employer branding
6. Professional Associations ICAI, ICSI, CII bodies for specialised professionals; subject-matter experts
7. E-Recruitment Web-based platforms | Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) | AI-powered screening | Online job boards
MERITS OF EXTERNAL
✓ Wider pool Access to best available talent nationally/globally
✓ Fresh ideas New perspectives, diversity of thought, external experience
✓ Specialised skills Access to expertise not available internally; niche capabilities
✓ No inbreeding Prevents cultural stagnation; challenges status quo
✓ Competitive benchmarking Market insights on compensation, best practices, industry standards
LIMITATIONS OF EXTERNAL
✗ High cost Advertising | Agency fees (15-25% for executive search) | Time commitment
✗ Time-consuming Longer hiring cycles | 4-8 weeks for mid-level | 8-16 weeks for senior
✗ Unknown candidate Higher risk | Background checks needed | Culture fit uncertain
✗ Staff demoralisation May demoralise current employees (outsider hired over promotion)
✗ High onboarding Greater training investment | Longer ramp-up time | Learning curve

⚖️ INTERNAL vs EXTERNAL - COMPREHENSIVE COMPARISON
PYQ Frequent Question | Strategic Choice | Situational Use
COMPARISON
INTERNAL: WHEN TO USE
✓ Replacement positions When vacant role is similar to existing capabilities
✓ Career development focus Organisation prioritises employee development and retention
✓ Stable workforce Low turnover; tight-knit team; cultural consistency priority
✓ Budget constraints Limited recruitment budget; need to minimise cost
✓ Urgent hiring Quick need for qualified person; time pressure
EXTERNAL: WHEN TO USE
✓ New/specialised roles Expertise not available internally; new skill requirements
✓ Growth/expansion phase Scaling up; need for fresh perspectives and external talent
✓ Cultural change needed Organisational transformation; need to challenge status quo
✓ Diversity/inclusion goals Achieving diversity targets; external pool essential
✓ Senior/leadership roles External leaders often preferred; fresh leadership perspective
INTEGRATED STRATEGY
Best Practice: HYBRID APPROACH Combine both sources strategically; use both for different scenarios
Internal First, External Second Internal recruitment builds morale | External for gaps | Balanced portfolio
Ratio based on context Stable org: 70% internal, 30% external | Growth org: 50-50 | Startups: 20% internal, 80% external
Flexibility required Adjust based on organisation strategy, business phase, and skills needed

🇮🇳 RECRUITMENT CHANNELS & INDIA CONTEXT
Sector-Specific Strategies | Government vs PSU vs Private | Gig Economy
INDIA CONTEXT
GOVERNMENT / UPSC RECRUITMENT
Model Centralised, transparent, merit-based examination system
Process UPSC CSE (Union Public Service Commission) national competitive exam
Timeline Very long: 2-3 years from exam to joining; multi-stage process
Reservations SC/ST/OBC/PwD/EWS quotas mandatory; strict compliance
Characteristic Highly formal, rule-bound, transparent but extremely time-consuming
PSU (Public Sector)
Model Corporate + Government hybrid; follows DoT guidelines
Process Recruitment through: Corporate guidelines | Public advertisement (mandatory) | Merit-based shortlisting
Timeline Long: 6-12 months typical; bureaucratic process
Reservations SC/ST/OBC quotas compliance; gender diversity targets
Typical companies BHEL, NTPC, ONGC, Bank of India, Indian Railways, HAL
PRIVATE SECTOR
Model Market-driven, competitive, performance-focused
Sources Mix: Campus (IIT/IIM) | Consultants (executive search) | Advertisement | Referrals
Timeline Fast: 4-8 weeks typical; quick decision-making; market-responsive
Focus Merit and cultural fit paramount; flexibility on sources; performance-based
Typical companies TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HUL, P&G, Accenture, Deloitte
STARTUPS & GIG ECONOMY
Startups Rapid recruitment for scaling | Campus + Consultants + Referrals | Equity incentives | Less formal
Gig Workers Zomato, Swiggy, Ola, Upwork: Digital platform-based | Algorithm matching | Rapid onboarding | Minimal training
E-Recruitment Trend AI-based CV screening | Algorithmic candidate matching | Digital platforms | Reduces bias but introduces new challenges
