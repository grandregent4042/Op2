External Environment
🌍 PESTLE Analysis
PESTLE Dimensions
Political: Policy changes, political stability, government intervention, regulations
Economic: GDP growth, inflation, interest rates, exchange rates, recession
Social: Demographics, cultural values, education levels, lifestyle trends
Technological: Innovation, AI adoption, digital transformation, automation
Legal: Labor laws, compliance requirements, intellectual property, regulations
Ecological: ESG mandates, climate change, sustainability requirements, carbon footprint
Environmental Complexity Models
Emery & Trist: Turbulent Fields
Environment moves from placid-randomized → disturbed-reactive → turbulent. Organizations must become more adaptive.
Duncan: Simple vs Complex Matrix
4 quadrants: Simple-Stable, Simple-Dynamic, Complex-Stable, Complex-Dynamic. Each requires different structure.
VUCA: World Context
Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, Ambiguity. Modern organizations operate in turbulent VUCA environments.

Topic 02 / Page 1
Organisational Goals
🎯 Goal Classifications
Goal Types
Primary vs Secondary
Primary: Core purpose (profit/service). Secondary: Support goals to achieve primary goals.
Official vs Operative
Official: Stated in mission. Operative: Actually pursued. Often gap between two.
Goal Pathologies
Goal Displacement (Merton): Means become ends. Following procedures becomes goal instead of achieving purpose.
Goal Succession (Selznick): Original goal achieved, organization pursues new goals to justify continued existence. Mission drift.
Red Tapism & Ritualism: Rigid adherence to rules. Form over substance. "We follow the rules" becomes goal.
Stakeholder Capitalism
Shareholder Primacy Shift: From shareholder-only focus to stakeholder balance
Triple Bottom Line: Profit + People + Planet considered together

Topic 02 / Page 2
MBO & Modern Variants
📊 Process & Critiques
MBO 5-Step Process (Drucker)
Step 1: Goal Setting at organizational level
Step 2: Cascading Objectives down hierarchy
Step 3: Joint Participation between manager and employee
Step 4: Periodic Review with feedback and adjustment
Step 5: Final Appraisal against agreed objectives
Critical Evaluation
Goodhart's Law: "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure." People game metrics instead of pursuing real goals.
Gaming & Short-termism: MBO focuses on quantifiable metrics, incentivizing short-term wins over long-term value.
Modern Variants
OKRs
Objectives & Key Results. Qualitative + measurable. Quarterly, stretch goals, transparent, agile.
Balanced Scorecard
Integrates 4 perspectives: Financial, Customer, Internal Process, Learning & Growth. Balanced view.

Evolution of Theory
📈 Classical School (1900-1930)
Key Contributors
Taylor (Scientific Management): Standardize tasks, time-motion studies, differential piece-rate wages. Maximize efficiency through analysis.
Fayol (Administrative Principles): 14 universal principles. Management functions: planning, organizing, commanding, coordinating, controlling.
Weber (Ideal Bureaucracy): Hierarchical authority, written rules, role-based positions, merit-based promotion. Rational-legal legitimacy.
Classical Assumptions
One best way to organize work exists
People are rational economic actors motivated by money
Managers plan; workers execute mechanically
Formal structure = actual behavior patterns
Specialization and standardization maximize efficiency

Topic 03 / Page 2
Neo-Classical & Systems
👥 Neo-Classical School (1930-1950)
Key Contributions
Hawthorne Studies (Mayo): Discovered informal groups and social needs matter more than incentives. Productivity increased due to attention and group dynamics.
Social Man Concept: Humans motivated by social needs, not just money
Informal Organisation exists alongside formal structure
Group norms override individual incentives
McGregor (Theory X & Y): Theory X assumes people dislike work, need control. Theory Y assumes people seek responsibility, self-motivated. Management style differs.
Systems Approach (1970+)
Open vs Closed Systems
Closed: Self-contained, minimal environment interaction. Open: Exchanges with environment, adapts, survives through adaptation.
Systems Concepts
Entropy & Negentropy
Entropy: System deteriorates without input. Negentropy: Input reverses deterioration.
Equifinality & Homeostasis
Equifinality: Multiple paths to same goal. Homeostasis: System maintains stability.
Cybernetics & Feedback: Information loops control and regulate system
