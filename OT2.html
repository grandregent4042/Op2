📋 NATURE & CONCEPT OF ORGANISATION
Core Definitions & Perspectives
NATURE & CONCEPT
CORE DEFINITIONS
Quote Directly "Collectivities of people established for pursuit of relatively specific objectives on continuous basis"
Key Implication Deliberate creation, rational coordination, closely relevant activities, attainment of goals
WESTERN PERSPECTIVE
Focus Formal structure, authority hierarchy, rational design
Emphasis Efficiency, specialization, clear roles and boundaries
Origin Classical & Neo-classical schools (Taylor, Fayol, Weber)
INDIAN PERSPECTIVE (PYQ 2022)
Focus Holistic approach, social responsibility, harmony with environment
Emphasis Community welfare, stakeholder interests, cultural values, ethical conduct
Difference Less hierarchical, more participative, considers human and social dimensions
DISTINGUISHING FEATURES OF ORGANIZATION
1. Deliberate Creation: Consciously created, not accidental
2. Specific Goals: Established for attainment of certain goals
3. Rational Coordination: Coordinated activities of closely relevant nature
4. Human Group: Composed of people working towards common objective
5. Continuous Basis: Operates on more or less continuous basis

🎯 ORGANISATIONAL GOALS - TYPES & PATHOLOGIES
Concepts & Critical Pathologies
ORG GOALS
GOAL TYPOLOGY
Primary Goals Main purpose of organization (profit, service delivery)
Secondary Goals Supporting objectives (employee welfare, social responsibility)
Official Goals Formally stated, public objectives
Operative Goals Actual goals pursued in practice (may differ from official)
Single & Multiple Single goal vs multiple goals pursued simultaneously
GOAL PATHOLOGIES
Goal Displacement Means become ends; E.g., bank officers lending to unviable borrowers for targets (NPA crisis)
Goal Succession New goal sought when original achieved/irrelevant; March of Dimes shifted from polio to birth defects
Ritualism Rule-following as end itself; endemic red-tapism in Indian public admin
Sub-goal Fixation Silo mentality; departmental goals primary at expense of overall goals
Goal Conflict Official vs operative (hospital: profit vs care); Stakeholder conflicts (shareholders vs employees)
IMPORTANCE STATEMENT
An org without goals "Like a rudderless ship" - lost direction, no purpose
Goal clarity Essential for organizational success and member commitment
CRITICAL PATHOLOGIES EXPLAINED
Goal Displacement (Merton): Process becomes more important than outcome; emphasis on achieving targets regardless of quality
Ritualism vs Goal Succession: Ritualism is rule-obsession with no goal; Goal Succession is shift to new goal after old one achieved
Coordination Failure: Sub-goal fixation prevents integration of departmental efforts towards unified organizational purpose

📊 MANAGEMENT BY OBJECTIVES (MBO) - DRUCKER 1954
5-Step Process & Comprehensive Analysis
MBO PROCESS
STEP 1: GOAL SETTING
Top Management Sets overall SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) strategic objectives
Scope Defines organizational vision, mission, and strategic direction
STEP 2: CASCADE
Hierarchy Org goals → Departmental → Section → Individual goals
Participation Each level participates in setting goals for level below
STEP 3: PARTICIPATION
Critical Feature Subordinates jointly agree on goals with managers (NOT top-down imposition)
Theory Y Link Builds ownership, commitment, and intrinsic motivation (McGregor)
STEP 4: PERIODIC REVIEW
Timing Progress reviews at agreed intervals (monthly/quarterly)
Adjustment Mid-course corrections possible; Emphasis on results, not activities
STEP 5: FINAL APPRAISAL
Evaluation Annual performance evaluation based on achieved goals
Basis Foundation for rewards, promotions, and development plans
MERITS OF MBO
Clarity: Clear expectations, Focus on results
Motivation: Participation builds ownership & commitment
Alignment: Individual goals integrated with organizational objectives
Accountability: Clear basis for performance evaluation
LIMITATIONS & PROBLEMS
Measurement Difficulty: Not all goals are easily measurable; Neglects qualitative aspects
Rigidity: Can create inflexible structures; Reduces organizational adaptability
Goal Displacement: Risk of means becoming ends; Overemphasis on quantifiable goals
Coaching Role: Challenging for managers to coach and develop subordinates effectively

📚 EVOLUTION OF ORG THEORY - CLASSICAL SCHOOL
Taylor, Fayol & Weber
CLASSICAL SCHOOL
TAYLOR (1911)
Scientific Management Time-motion studies, standardization of work methods
Focus Shop-floor efficiency; Worker productivity through science
Assumption Economic Man - workers motivated solely by wages
Methods Scientific selection & training of workers
FAYOL (1916)
14 Principles Division of Labour, Authority-Responsibility, Discipline, Unity of Command, Unity of Direction, Subordination to General Interest, Scalar Chain, Esprit de Corps
5 Functions (POCCC) Planning, Organizing, Commanding, Coordinating, Controlling
Key Contribution First to identify management as universal function (unlike Taylor's manufacturing focus)
Unity of Command One superior for each subordinate - CONTRADICTS Taylor's functional foremanship
WEBER (1922)
Ideal Bureaucracy Rational-legal authority system
Characteristics Hierarchical structure, Standardized rules, Task specialization, Meritocratic selection
Benefits Efficiency, fairness, predictability, impersonality
CLASSICAL SCHOOL DEBATE
Unity of Command Issue: Fayol's one-superior principle (unity of command) directly contradicts Taylor's functional foremanship (8 specialists reporting to worker)
Fundamental Difference: Fayol advocates unified authority; Taylor advocates specialized expertise - major classical school debate
CLASSICAL SCHOOL LIMITATIONS
• Ignores informal organization
• Neglects human needs and social dimensions
• Assumes economic motivation only
• Does not account for environmental contingencies

👥 NEO-CLASSICAL / HUMAN RELATIONS THEORY
Hawthorne Studies & Core Propositions
NEO-CLASSICAL
HAWTHORNE STUDIES (1924-1932)
Researchers Elton Mayo & Roethlisberger
Discovery Informal groups set norms that OVERRIDE formal rules
Finding Productivity increased due to attention paid, not work conditions
Key Insight Non-economic factors powerful in motivation
CORE PROPOSITIONS
Social Man Workers motivated by belonging, recognition, peer relationships (NOT just wages)
Informal Org Exists alongside formal structure; sets own norms that OVERRIDE formal rules
Participation In decisions satisfies social needs, builds commitment, improves outcomes (counters classical top-down)
Non-Economic Rewards Recognition, status, belonging, group acceptance powerful motivators without pay increases
Small Groups Primary unit of social organization; managers must understand & work with informal groups
CRITICISMS
Cynical Puppeteering (UPSC 2016) Management manipulation - making workers feel good to maximize productivity
Trifling Empirics Hawthorne findings methodologically flawed (small samples, observer effects, researcher bias)
Ignores Formal Swings to extreme opposite of classical - ignores formal structure entirely
Pro-Management Bias Assumes management & worker interests reconcilable - ignores structural power asymmetry (Marxist critique)
CRITICISMS EXPLAINED
"Cynical Puppeteering": Critics argue Mayo's model = management tool for control, not genuine empowerment
"Trifling Body of Empirical Info": Cannot generalize from small Hawthorne sample; methodologically unsound
Theoretical Balance: Neither classical (ignores informal) nor neo-classical (ignores formal) alone is complete

🔄 SYSTEMS APPROACH & CONTINGENCY THEORY
Katz & Kahn (1966) & Contingency Core Principle
SYSTEMS & CONTINGENCY
SYSTEMS APPROACH
Katz & Kahn (1966) Organization as OPEN SYSTEM interacting with environment
Model Input → Process → Output → Feedback
Key Features Interdependence of organizational parts; Maintains equilibrium; Adapts to environment
CONTINGENCY CORE PRINCIPLE
Central Tenet "Optimal structure, process & management style is CONTINGENT on situational variables"
No Universal Best Way No single approach works for all organizations in all situations
Key Variables Environment (stable vs turbulent), Technology, Size, Strategy
KEY CONTINGENCY RESEARCHERS
Burns & Stalker (1961) Stable → Mechanistic (formal, hierarchical, centralized)
Organic Structures Turbulent → Organic (flexible, decentralized, lateral communication)
Lawrence & Lorsch (1967) Differentiation & integration must match environmental demands
Woodward (1965) Technology determines structure: Unit → flat; Mass prod → hierarchical; Process → flat
BURNS & STALKER FRAMEWORK
Mechanistic Structures: Formal rules, hierarchical authority, centralized decision-making, narrow specialization - suit STABLE environments
Organic Structures: Flexible roles, decentralized decisions, lateral communication, broad skills - suit TURBULENT environments
WOODWARD'S TECHNOLOGY-STRUCTURE LINK
Unit Production: Small batches, custom design → Flat, organic structure, high worker discretion
Mass Production: High volume, standardized → Hierarchical, mechanistic, rigid procedures
Process Production: Continuous flow → Flat structure, focus on maintenance, automated systems

🎓 ORGANIZATION THEORY EVOLUTION - SUMMARY
Comparative Analysis & Key Takeaways
THEORY EVOLUTION
CLASSICAL SCHOOL
Pioneers Taylor, Fayol, Weber
Focus Structure, efficiency, formal authority, rules
Assumption Economic man; Rational decision-making
Major Gap Ignores informal organization and human needs
NEO-CLASSICAL
Pioneer Elton Mayo & Hawthorne Studies
Focus Informal org, human relations, social needs
Assumption Social man; Non-economic motivators matter
Major Gap Swings too far opposite - ignores formal structure & power asymmetry
SYSTEMS & CONTINGENCY
Pioneers Katz & Kahn, Burns & Stalker, Woodward, Lawrence & Lorsch
Focus Organization as open system; Contingent design approach
Core Principle No "one best way" - structure contingent on environment & technology
Contribution Integrates both formal & informal; Accounts for environmental dynamics
EVOLUTION LOGIC
Classical: Focuses on structure & efficiency; Assumes rationality
Neo-Classical: Corrects classical neglect of human side; BUT overcorrects by ignoring formal structure
Systems/Contingency: Integrates both perspectives; Adds environmental dimension for realistic organizational understanding
KEY EXAM POINTERS
Compare perspectives: Always highlight differences in assumptions, focus areas, and contributions
Unity of Command debate: Fayol vs Taylor - key classical school controversy
Hawthorne criticisms: Know both "cynical puppeteering" & "trifling empirics" criticisms
Contingency principle: No "one best way" - master this concept for exam success
