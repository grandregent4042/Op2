Design Challenges
⚙️ Core Tensions
Differentiation Dimensions
Horizontal
Specialization by function or product. HR, Finance, Ops, Marketing.
Vertical
Hierarchy levels. CEO → Managers → Supervisors → Workers.
Spatial
Geographic distribution. Head office, regional, plant, field locations.
Integration Mechanisms
Hierarchy of Authority: Escalate decisions upward
Liaison Roles: People connecting different functions
Task Forces: Temporary cross-functional teams
Integrating Departments: Staff units coordinating silos
Centralisation vs Decentralisation
Core Issue: Location of decision-making authority. Centralized = top controls; Decentralized = distributed authority. Trade-offs between consistency and responsiveness.
Delegation Principles guide authority distribution
Digital Paradox: IT enables real-time control even with decentralization
Standardisation vs Mutual Adjustment
Formal Rules vs Informal Norms: Procedures vs flexibility
Standardisation of Outputs/Skills: Consistency vs specialization
Direct Communication: Person-to-person coordination without hierarchy

Structural Typologies
🏗️ Mechanistic vs Organic
Mechanistic Structure
Stable Environment Fit
Tall hierarchy, rigid rules, narrow span, centralized authority. Efficient for standardized work. Slow to adapt.
Organic Structure
Dynamic Environment Fit
Flat hierarchy, flexible roles, wide span, decentralized authority. Adaptive, innovative. Coordination challenges.
Mintzberg's 5 Configurations
Simple Structure
Centralized, minimal staff. Small companies, startups. Owner-run.
Machine Bureaucracy
Standardized processes, many rules. Manufacturing, PSUs. Efficient but inflexible.
Professional Bureaucracy
Expertise-driven, professional standards. Hospitals, universities, law firms.
Divisionalised Form
Multiple divisions, profit centers. Large corporations, conglomerates. M-form.
Adhocracy
Project-based, flexible. Consultancies, tech startups, R&D units. Innovative.
Formal vs Informal Organisation
Formal System
Positional authority, official channels, planned structure, documented rules
Informal System
Spontaneous emergence, grapevine communication, social cohesion, unwritten norms

Contingency Factors
📊 Situational Variables
Environmental Uncertainty
Core Principle: Stable environments suit mechanistic structures. Dynamic environments need organic structures. Uncertainty requires flexibility.
Technology Factor
Routine vs Non-Routine
Routine technology (assembly lines) → mechanistic. Non-routine (R&D, creative) → organic. Technology determines coordination needs.
Organisational Size
Small orgs: Simple structure, direct coordination
Medium orgs: Functional or matrix structures
Large orgs: Divisional or complex matrix
Size affects specialization, standardization, hierarchy levels
Strategy Factor
Structure Follows Strategy: Chandler's insight. Organization design should align with competitive strategy. Cost-leadership → functional. Diversification → divisional.
Differentiation strategy → organic, flexible structure
Cost-leadership strategy → mechanistic, standardized structure
Focus strategy → specialized, mission-driven structure

Modern Perspectives
🌐 Contemporary Design
Ambidextrous Organisations
Concept: Balance exploration (innovation, new products) and exploitation (efficiency, current products). Separate units with different structures, coordinated at top.
Example: 3M has 70% efficiency teams and 30% innovation labs
IT-Enabled Design
Digital Command & Control
Real-time data dashboards enable managers to monitor and control operations from distance. Flatter structures possible with IT support.
Network & Platform Models
Network Organisations: Decentralized, connected through IT and relationships. Minimal hierarchy. Examples: Uber, Airbnb, Upwork.
Platform Organisations: Core platform with ecosystem of partners. Algorithmic coordination replaces management. Scalable, low cost.
Key Insight
Modern Paradox: Technology allows both more control (surveillance) and more autonomy (remote work). Organizations must choose design carefully based on strategy and culture.
