🏛️ ORGANISATIONAL DESIGN - MEANING & 4 BASIC CHALLENGES
Concept & Key Distinction
ORG DESIGN
DEFINITION
Gareth Jones (2013) "Process by which managers select and manage aspects of structure and culture so that an organization can control activities necessary to achieve its goals"
PYQ 2025 (20M) "Org design is not merely about structure, but about strategically aligning people, processes, and objectives in an ever-changing environment"
KEY DISTINCTION
Structure Snapshot of authority relationships at a point in time (static view)
Design Ongoing adaptive process - as strategy, environment, and technology change, design must change too (dynamic view)
Implication Design is continuous management, not one-time decision
4 BASIC CHALLENGES (GARETH JONES - MUST USE)
Challenge 1 Differentiation - how to divide tasks, activities, and authority
Challenge 2 Integration - how to coordinate and integrate differentiated parts
Challenge 3 Centralization vs Decentralization - where to locate decision-making authority
Challenge 4 Standardization vs Mutual Adjustment - how to control and coordinate work
RELATED CHALLENGES
Challenge 5 Formal vs Informal Organization - coordinating both
Challenge 6 Mechanistic vs Organic Structures - matching with environment
STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT CONCEPT (PYQ 2025)
Not merely structure: Goes beyond formal authority relationships
Three dimensions: People (culture, motivation, capabilities), Processes (workflows, systems), Objectives (goals, strategy)
Ever-changing: Must continuously adapt to environmental shifts, technological changes, strategic evolution

🔀 DIFFERENTIATION & INTEGRATION (LAWRENCE & LORSCH 1967)
Contingency Research & Key Findings
DIFF & INTEG
TYPES OF DIFFERENTIATION
Horizontal Diff Division of labour by functions (Marketing, Finance, Operations)
Vertical Diff Division of authority by hierarchy levels (Top mgmt, Middle, Supervisory)
Spatial Diff Division by geography (regional offices, departments, branches)
Degree of Diff Low to High - depends on environmental uncertainty (contingency)
LAWRENCE & LORSCH RESEARCH
Study Design 3 industries: Plastics (dynamic, high uncertainty), Food (moderate), Containers (stable, low uncertainty)
KEY FINDING High-performing DYNAMIC env had MORE differentiation AND proportionally more integration
Stable Env Firms High-performing had LESS of both differentiation & integration
Core Implication Environmental uncertainty determines optimal differentiation level; integration must MATCH degree of differentiation - complementary imperatives
PROBLEMS OF EXCESSIVE DIFFERENTIATION
Communication Barriers between departments; Language differences; Isolated teams
Silo Mentality Each department focused only on own goals; Loss of unified purpose
Coordination Cost Explosion of integration mechanisms needed; Resource drain
Role Conflict Unclear responsibilities; Power struggles between departments
Loss of Synergy Knowledge trapped in silos; Missed opportunities for collaboration
MINTZBERG'S 6 COORDINATION MECHANISMS
1. Mutual Adjustment Informal communication between team members
2. Direct Supervision Manager coordinates work directly
3. Standardization of Work Specify how work should be done (procedures, rules)
4. Standardization of Outputs Specify what results are expected (targets, standards)
5. Standardization of Skills Training ensures people coordinate without explicit coordination
6. Standardization of Norms Culture, shared values ensure coordination through shared understanding
CONTINGENCY INSIGHT - CRITICAL FOR EXAMS
Dynamic/Uncertain Environments: Need MORE specialization (differentiation) BUT must invest proportionally MORE in integration mechanisms (teams, committees, liaisons)
Stable Environments: Can function with LESS specialization and LESS integration - simple hierarchy sufficient
Balance Critical: Differentiation without integration = chaos; Integration without differentiation = inefficiency

⚖️ CENTRALISATION & DECENTRALISATION
Delegation vs Decentralization & Principles
CENT & DECENT
DELEGATION VS DECENTRALISATION
Delegation Assignment of specific task/authority to subordinate for limited period; Remains temporary; Manager can reclaim
Decentralization Permanent distribution of authority across organizational levels; Structural feature; Part of design
Key Difference Delegation = tactical/temporary; Decentralization = strategic/structural (PYQ 2023)
PRINCIPLES OF DELEGATION
Parity Principle Authority delegated MUST be commensurate with responsibility assigned - cannot hold someone responsible without authority to fulfil it
Absoluteness of Accountability Accountability CANNOT be delegated - delegating manager remains accountable to superior even after delegating to subordinates
Scalar Principle Clear chain of authority from top to bottom must be maintained - every person must know to whom they report
Unity of Command Each subordinate should receive orders from only ONE superior (Fayol) - avoids role conflict
Management by Exception Only exceptional matters should be referred upward - routine decisions handled at delegated level
FACTORS DETERMINING DEGREE OF DECENTRALISATION
Environmental Complexity Complex/turbulent environment → Decentralize; Stable/simple → Centralize
Organization Size Larger orgs → Decentralize; Small orgs → Centralize
Subordinate Competence High competence → Decentralize; Low competence → Centralize
Technology Routine technology → Centralize; Complex/non-routine → Decentralize
Organizational Culture Trust-based culture → Decentralize; Control-oriented → Centralize
Strategic Goals Innovation/responsiveness → Decentralize; Efficiency/control → Centralize
ACCOUNTABILITY VS AUTHORITY - CRITICAL CONCEPT
Parity Principle Issue: Holding someone responsible without giving authority = unfair & counterproductive
Absoluteness Principle Issue: Manager cannot escape accountability by delegating - ensures manager supervises properly
Practical Implication: Manager must ensure delegated authority matches delegated responsibility AND maintain oversight

📋 STANDARDISATION vs MUTUAL ADJUSTMENT & RULES vs NORMS
Control & Coordination Methods
STANDARD & RULES
FORMALISATION / STANDARDISATION
Definition Degree to which organizational roles, procedures, and processes are explicitly defined and documented
Characteristics Written rules, Standard procedures, Formal job descriptions, Documented workflows
Advantages Consistency, Predictability, Control, Reduces ambiguity, Training easier
Disadvantages Inflexibility, Over-bureaucratization, Stifles creativity, Red-tapism, Slow adaptation
MUTUAL ADJUSTMENT
Definition Coordination through informal communication and ad-hoc adjustment between team members
Characteristics No formal rules, Flexible, Relies on communication, Adaptive, Role ambiguity
Advantages Flexibility, Innovation, Quick adaptation, Employee autonomy, Responsiveness
Disadvantages Inconsistency, Ambiguity, Requires high coordination effort, Role conflicts, Difficult to control
RULES vs NORMS (PYQ 2019 - 20M)
Rules Formal, explicit, written requirements - "Thou shalt NOT"; Enforced by authority; Breach has consequences
Norms Informal, implicit, unwritten expectations - "We do things this way"; Enforced by peer pressure; Internalized by members
Key Difference Rules = formal control; Norms = informal control; Norms are MORE powerful in practice
Importance from Design Perspective Designing org requires considering both - organizations run on shared norms as much as formal rules
WHEN TO USE WHICH
High Standardization Routine work, safety-critical, compliance-heavy, stable environment, large organization
High Mutual Adjustment Complex work, innovation-focused, dynamic environment, small teams, knowledge workers
Mixed Approach Most organizations need BOTH - core processes standardized, peripheral flexible
RULES vs NORMS - ORGANIZATIONAL REALITY
Power of Norms: Organizational culture & informal norms often determine actual behavior MORE than formal rules
Design Challenge: Managers must design both formal rules AND foster positive informal norms aligned with organizational goals
PYQ Focus: Question may ask to explain importance of rules vs norms from organizational design perspective

🔀 FORMAL & INFORMAL ORGANISATION - COORDINATING BOTH
Integration & Strategic Management
FORMAL & INFORMAL
FORMAL ORGANISATION
Definition Deliberately created structure with defined roles, authority, and procedures
Characteristics Written rules, Hierarchy, Job descriptions, Authority relationships, Formal communication
Purpose Control, coordination, and achievement of organizational objectives
INFORMAL ORGANISATION
Definition Natural social structure that emerges from personal relationships and interactions
Characteristics Unwritten norms, Informal leaders, Social networks, Peer relationships, Grapevine
Purpose Meet social needs, fill gaps in formal structure, provide identity and belonging
WHY COORDINATE BOTH (PYQ 2024)
Fills Gaps Informal org fills gaps where formal rules/procedures absent - informal norms govern behavior (Hawthorne Bank Wiring Room finding)
Informal Leaders Can support OR undermine formal authority - ignoring them is strategically dangerous
Grapevine Spreads info faster than formal channels - managers who ignore it lose ground reality contact
Change Management Managing informal leaders = managing change effectively
COORDINATION STRATEGY
Legitimise Informal Leaders Recognize and give formal status to influential informal leaders
Involve in Decisions Include informal leaders in formal decision-making processes
Use Social Capital Leverage their influence for change management and organizational goals
Align Rewards Ensure formal reward systems align with informal group norms and values
Proactive Grapevine Use Can be used proactively to communicate during change and organizational transitions
HAWTHORNE BANK WIRING ROOM - KEY FINDING
Discovery: Informal group norms set production quotas that DIFFERED from formal organizational targets
Implication: Informal organization was more powerful than formal rules in controlling actual behavior
Design Lesson: Organizational design MUST account for and work WITH informal organization, not ignore it
STRATEGIC IMPORTANCE (PYQ 2024)
Cannot be Ignored: Informal organization is inevitable & powerful force in every organization
Change Management: Success of organizational change depends on gaining informal leaders' support
Design Implication: Formal design must complement informal organization, not fight it

⚙️ MECHANISTIC vs ORGANIC STRUCTURES - BURNS & STALKER 1961
Environmental Fit & Structural Design
MECH & ORGANIC
MECHANISTIC STRUCTURE
Characteristics Formal, hierarchical, centralized, standardized procedures, narrow specialization
Decision-Making Centralized at top; Limited subordinate discretion; Top-down authority
Communication Vertical (up-down), Formal channels, Limited lateral communication
Coordination Through hierarchy, rules, and direct supervision
Suitable For Stable environments, routine tasks, efficiency focus, cost leadership strategy
ORGANIC STRUCTURE
Characteristics Flexible, decentralized, loosely defined roles, broad specialization
Decision-Making Decentralized; Distributed authority; Subordinate discretion; Participative
Communication Lateral (across), Informal channels, Lateral networks important
Coordination Through mutual adjustment, team collaboration, shared goals
Suitable For Turbulent environments, complex tasks, innovation focus, differentiation strategy
KEY ENVIRONMENTAL FITS
STABLE ENVIRONMENT → Mechanistic structure optimal; Predictable, standardized approach works; Efficiency valued
TURBULENT ENVIRONMENT → Organic structure optimal; Flexibility needed, rapid adaptation required; Innovation valued
Mismatch Problems Mechanistic in turbulent = rigid, slow, unresponsive; Organic in stable = inefficient, chaotic
COMPARATIVE FEATURES
Knowledge Flow Mechanistic: Vertical; Organic: Lateral & across boundaries
Job Descriptions Mechanistic: Precise, narrow; Organic: Broad, flexible
Authority Source Mechanistic: Position/formal; Organic: Expertise/knowledge
Problem-Solving Mechanistic: Refer up; Organic: Solve locally, collaborate
CONTINGENCY PRINCIPLE - CRITICAL EXAM CONCEPT
No "One Best Way": Neither structure is universally "best" - depends on environment
Environmental Determinism: Structure must FIT environmental characteristics for organizational effectiveness
Design Choice: Managers must diagnose environment THEN choose appropriate structure - not design in vacuum
TRANSITION CHALLENGE
Problem: Many organizations trapped in mechanistic structures even as environments become turbulent (legacy structure)
Modern Need: Need for hybrid structures - mechanistic core (efficiency) with organic adaptability (innovation)
Design Evolution: Contemporary organizations moving toward more organic structures due to increasing environmental turbulence

📊 ORG DESIGN CHALLENGES - INTEGRATED SUMMARY
All 6 Challenges & Interrelationships
6 DESIGN CHALLENGES
CHALLENGE 1: DIFFERENTIATION
Nature How to divide tasks, activities, and authority
Types Horizontal (by function), Vertical (by hierarchy), Spatial (by geography)
Contingency More differentiation in complex/turbulent environments
CHALLENGE 2: INTEGRATION
Nature How to coordinate & integrate differentiated parts
Mechanisms 6 mechanisms from Mintzberg (mutual adjustment to norms)
Critical Link Integration level MUST match differentiation level (Lawrence & Lorsch)
CHALLENGE 3: CENTRALIZATION
Nature Where to locate decision-making authority
Key Principles Parity, Accountability, Scalar Chain, Unity of Command, Exception
Factors Environment, size, competence, technology, culture, strategy
CHALLENGE 4: STANDARDISATION
Nature How to control & coordinate work
Spectrum Standardization (rules, procedures) ↔ Mutual Adjustment (informal coordination)
Balance Most orgs need mix - core standardized, periphery flexible
CHALLENGE 5: FORMAL & INFORMAL
Nature Coordinating both formal structure and informal organization
Key Insight Informal org fills gaps, provides social needs, has power over behavior
Strategy Legitimize informal leaders, use in change management, align rewards
CHALLENGE 6: MECHANISTIC/ORGANIC
Nature Matching structure with environmental requirements
Fit Logic Stable → Mechanistic; Turbulent → Organic
Modern Challenge Need hybrid structures - mechanistic efficiency + organic adaptability
INTERRELATIONSHIP OF CHALLENGES
Differentiation → Integration Need: More differentiation requires proportionally more integration
Environment → Centralization: Turbulent environments require decentralization for responsiveness
Standardization ↔ Mutual Adjustment: Must balance formal control with informal flexibility
All → Mechanistic/Organic: Ultimate result is either mechanistic or organic structure depending on how challenges are resolved

Topic "Rules are different from norms - explain importance from org design perspective"
Key Points Rules = formal, written, enforced by authority; Norms = informal, implicit, enforced by peers; Norms more powerful in practice; Design must consider both
