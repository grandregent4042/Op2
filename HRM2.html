⛓️ JOB ANALYSIS CHAIN - Master Framework
Foundation Stone of HRM (Flippo) | PYQ 2013-2025
JOB ANALYSIS CHAIN
4-STEP FRAMEWORK
Step 1: JOB ANALYSIS Data collection process; systematic collection of job facts about operations and responsibilities
Step 2: ↓ JD + JS JD (WHAT - objective) + JS (WHO - subjective)
Step 3: ↓ JOB EVALUATION Determines job worth for internal equity and pay structure
Step 4: ↓ CASCADES Flows to: Recruitment >> Selection >> Training >> Appraisal >> Compensation
DEFINITIONS (Memorise Flippo)
Edwin Flippo (MOST COMPREHENSIVE) "Process of studying and collecting information relating to operations and responsibilities of specific job"
Gary Dessler "Procedure through which you determine duties of job positions and characteristics of people to hire"
Dale Yoder "Procedure by which facts with respect to each job are secured, organised and combined"
PYQ 2020 (KEY LINE) "Job analysis is mainly and essentially a data collection process"
NATURE & CHARACTERISTICS
Data-centric Systematically gathers facts about job content, context, requirements
Job-focused NOT person-focused Analyses the job, not the incumbent; what job demands, not who holds it
Dynamic Jobs change; JA must be periodically updated
Foundation function ALL HRM activities depend on accurate JA data
Tripartite scope Job content + Job context + Job requirements (KSAs)

🔬 JOB ANALYSIS METHODS - 6 METHODS
Data Collection Techniques | PYQ 2020, 2016
6 JA METHODS
METHOD 1-2
OBSERVATION Analyst watches; records tasks, pace, conditions. Best: Manual, repetitive, physical. Limitation: Observer effect (Hawthorne)
INTERVIEW Q&A with jobholder & supervisor. Best: Supervisory, knowledge, complex. Limitation: Bias, time-intensive
METHOD 3-4
QUESTIONNAIRE Standardised written instrument. Best: Large orgs, multiple similar jobs. Limitation: Response quality, literacy
DIARY/LOG Employee self-records tasks/time. Best: R&D, scientific, irregular. Limitation: Subjective, inflation
METHOD 5-6
CRITICAL INCIDENT TECHNIQUE (CIT) Collect effective/ineffective behaviours. Best: Performance appraisal, BARS. Limitation: Focuses on extremes
PAQ 195 items, 6 divisions. Best: Blue-collar, non-managerial. Limit: Not for knowledge workers
PAQ vs MPDQ (PYQ 2020)
PAQ (McCormick, Purdue) 195 items | 6 divisions | Non-managerial | Blue-collar | Production | Compensation benchmarking
MPDQ (Tornow & Pinto) 197 items | 13 categories | Managerial | Leadership | Succession planning | Executive assessment

📄 JOB DESCRIPTION (JD) - WHAT THE JOB IS
DAECC Framework | 10 Components | Yoder's "Word Picture"
JOB DESCRIPTION
JD DEFINITION & NATURE
Definition "Written statement describing job title, reporting relationship, duties, responsibilities, working conditions"
Yoder's "Word picture" Of organisational relationships and functions of job
Nature OBJECTIVE | Job-focused (not person-focused) | Written from job's perspective | STABLE
Answers: "What does job entail?" NOT "Who can do it?" (that's JS)
DAECC FRAMEWORK (EXAM CRITICAL)
D - DEFINITION Job title, reporting relationship, objective, department
A - ACTIVITIES Major duties & responsibilities with time allocation; 5-8 key tasks
E - ENVIRONMENT Working conditions, hazards, physical demands, shift work, stress
C - COMPETENCY Knowledge, skills, abilities, behaviours required (KSAOs)
C - COMPENSATION Salary grade, pay range, benefits bracket
JD 10 COMPONENTS
1-2. Job Title & Code/Grade Clear name; classification for compensation
3-4. Reporting & Department To whom reports; organisational unit and location
5-6. Objective & Responsibilities Why exists (2-3 sentences); major tasks with time allocation
7-8. Authority & KPIs Decision-making power; measurable outcomes
9-10. Competencies & Reporting Skills/knowledge needed; if supervisory, who reports to this position

👤 JOB SPECIFICATION (JS) - WHO SHOULD DO THE JOB
9 Components | JD vs JS Distinction (EXAM CRITICAL)
JOB SPECIFICATION
JS DEFINITION & NATURE
Definition "Written statement describing minimum qualifications, skills, abilities, experience required"
Yoder: "Minimum qualities" Characteristics required for satisfactory performance
Nature SUBJECTIVE | Candidate-focused | Written from candidate's perspective | EVOLVES
Answers: "Who can do it?" Describes candidate qualifications, not the job itself
JS 9 COMPONENTS
1-2. Education & Experience Minimum degree/certification; years in related roles
3-4. Technical Skills & Language Tools, software, systems; proficiency levels
5-6. Physical & Psychological Strength, mobility, sensory; temperament, stress tolerance
7-8. Personality & Demands Teamwork, communication, leadership; travel, shift work
9. Special Requirements Certifications, security clearance, driving license
JD vs JS - DISTINCTION
JD: WHAT JOB IS Objective | Job-focused | Job's perspective | Stable | Describes content, tasks, responsibilities
JS: WHO CAN DO IT Subjective | Candidate-focused | Candidate's perspective | Evolves | Describes qualifications
Relationship Both from Job Analysis | Different purposes | JD >> Performance standards | JS >> Recruitment/Selection
Example JD: "Manage 10 people; deliver projects on schedule/budget" vs JS: "MBA; 7+ yrs PM; PMP certification"

💰 JOB EVALUATION - DETERMINING JOB WORTH
4 Methods | Point Factor (Most Popular) | Internal Equity
JOB EVALUATION
JE DEFINITION & OBJECTIVES
Definition "Process of determining relative worth/value of jobs within organisation to establish equitable wage/salary structure"
Key: Compares JOBS (NOT people) Purpose: Establish INTERNAL EQUITY
6 Objectives Establish equity | Compensation basis | Legal compliance | Support HR planning | Career planning | Improve morale
JE vs PA (CRITICAL) JE evaluates JOB (worth) | PA evaluates PERSON (performance) | Both needed: JE determines range; PA determines position
METHOD 1-2: Non-Quantitative
RANKING Jobs ranked 1st, 2nd, 3rd by overall worth. Simplest, quickest. Advantage: Easy, low cost. Limitation: Subjective
CLASSIFICATION Pre-established grades (A, B, C, D, E); jobs assigned to best-fit. Used: Government, large orgs. Advantage: Clear. Limitation: Rigid
METHOD 3: POINT FACTOR (MOST POPULAR)
8-Step Process Identify factors | Define scales | Weight factors (%) | Assign points | Assess jobs | Calculate total | Group grades | Set ranges
Typical Factors & Weights Skill (30%) | Responsibility (35%) | Effort (15%) | Conditions (10%) | Impact (10%)
Example Senior Systems Engineer: Skill 45 + Responsibility 40 + Effort 15 + Conditions 12 + Impact 13 = 125 pts >> Grade C >> $80k-$95k
Advantages Objective | Systematic | Defensible | Transparent | Scalable | Legal defensibility
METHOD 4: FACTOR COMPARISON
Process Reference/benchmark jobs (known pay) compared; others compared proportionally
Advantage Direct link to market pay; refined; realistic compensation
Limitation Complex; requires accurate benchmarks; difficult if markets shift

⚠️ CHALLENGES & INDIA CONTEXT
R&D Challenges (PYQ 2018) | Workers' Reluctance (PYQ 2016) | Current Affairs
CHALLENGES
R&D JA CHALLENGES (PYQ 2018 - 20M)
Not observable Cognitive/creative work cannot be timed or watched like manual work
Unpredictable tasks Non-routine, unique per project; no standard task list
Intangible output Ideas, discoveries, patents; quality hard to define in advance
Professional resistance Scientists view JA as bureaucratic intrusion on intellectual work
Analyst knowledge gap HR analysts may lack domain expertise
Solutions Diary/Log | CIT | Competency Mapping | Peer Review | Flexible output-based JD
WORKERS' RELUCTANCE (PYQ 2016)
Fear of job evaluation consequences Accurate JA may reveal lower complexity >> lower pay grade
Fear of job redundancy Documenting tasks may expose overstaffing or automation risks
Perception of surveillance JA seen as management control tool, not help
Remedies Participative approach | Clear communication | Confidentiality | Union involvement
INDIA CONTEXT & CURRENT AFFAIRS
Government Rigid classification (Group A-D) | Mission Karmayogi shift to role-based JDs
PSU Classification + Point Factor hybrid | Formal process for JD amendments
Private Sector Point Factor most common | IT shifting to competency/skill-based JDs
IT Trend Infosys, Wipro: rigid task-based >> competency-based JDs (more agile)
Gig Workers Swiggy, Zomato, Ola: challenge to define gig 'jobs' | Implications for JA/JD/JS and social security
