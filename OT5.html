Culture Concepts
🎭 Core Definitions
Key Definitions
Schein: "Pattern of basic assumptions created, invented or discovered by a group as it learns to cope with problems of external adaptation and internal integration."
Robbins: "Shared system of meaning and understanding that determines largely how employees act. System of shared values that define what is important."
Deal & Kennedy: "The way we do things around here." Culture is how people behave when nobody is watching.
Schein's 3-Level Model
Level 1: Artifacts (Visible)
Office design, dress code, language, symbols, logos, ceremonies, stories, legends, heroes. Visible but hard to interpret correctly.
Level 2: Espoused Values (Conscious)
What organization says it values: integrity, innovation, teamwork. Often aspirational. May not match behavior.
Level 3: Basic Assumptions (Unconscious)
What organization truly believes (often unconscious). Deepest level. Shapes actual behavior. "People can't be trusted", "Hierarchy is natural", "Only profit matters."
Culture vs Climate
Culture
Deep, enduring, causal. Years to change. Defines what's important.
Climate
Surface, perceptions, effect. Changes with leadership. How it feels to work here.

Culture Formation
🔧 Creation & Sustenance
Creation Process
Founders Role: Seeding initial values. Founders' personalities and philosophies shape early culture
Hiring: Cultural fit. Selection process reinforces desired culture
Critical Incidents: How organization responds to crises reveals true values
Sustaining Mechanisms
Selection Practices: Hire for cultural fit, not just skills
Top Management Behaviour: Leaders model culture more than policies
Socialisation (3 phases): Pre-arrival (expectations), Encounter (reality), Metamorphosis (integration)
Stories/Rituals/Symbols: Narratives embed and transmit cultural values
Culture as Competitive Advantage
VRIN Framework
Valuable: Culture creates value. Rare: Not easily copied. Inimitable: Hard to replicate. Non-substitutable: No easy alternatives. When all met, competitive advantage.
Positive Impacts
Social Control & Coordination: Norms guide behavior without constant supervision
Identity & Commitment: Strong culture builds belonging and loyalty
Competitive Advantage: Unique culture hard to copy, source of sustained advantage

Managing Culture
⚠️ Negative Impacts
Strategic Challenges
Barrier to Change: Strong cultures resist new strategies. "This is how we do things" prevents adaptation to new market realities.
Diversity & Inclusion Issues: Homogeneous cultures exclude minorities. Same-person hiring perpetuates bias. Glass ceilings institutionalized.
Groupthink & Innovation Suppression: Conformity pressure stifles dissent. Risk-aversion kills innovation. "Fitting in" valued over "challenging norms."
Ethical Pathologies: Enron culture valued profits over ethics. Company cultures can normalize unethical behavior. Compliance without conscience.
M&A Value Destruction: Cultural clashes in mergers destroy value. "We're being acquired by a company with a different culture" = exodus of talent.
🇮🇳 Indian Context Challenges: High hierarchical deference (Hofstede's PDI), colonial bureaucratic legacy in PSUs, risk aversion, glass ceilings for gender/caste minorities, command-and-control cultures.
Way Forward
Mission Karmayogi: India's civil service reform for modern culture
Psychological Safety: Google's Project Aristotle finding: Safe to speak up = high performance
ESG & Diversity Mandates: Regulatory push for inclusive cultures

Topic 02 / Page 2
Managing Change
🔄 Culture Change Process
Change Triggers
Strategic Discontinuity: New strategy requires new culture
Performance or Ethical Crises: Crisis forces cultural reset
Leadership Change: New leader brings new values
Levers for Culture Change
Leadership Modelling
New leader visibly demonstrates new values. Actions speak louder than words. Changes what gets rewarded.
Reward System Redesign
Change what's measured and rewarded. If teamwork not rewarded, culture won't change to collaborative.
Structural Changes
Reorganize to force new interactions and relationships. Break up old silos.
New Narratives & Heroes
Tell new stories. Highlight role models embodying new culture. Retire old heroes.
Failure Reasons
Unconscious Resistance: People don't consciously resist, but old assumptions persist. "We tried that before, it didn't work."
Time Horizon Mismatch: Culture change takes years. Leaders expect results in quarters. Patience runs out before change takes hold.
Espoused vs Enacted Gap: Organization espouses new values but rewards old behavior. "We value teamwork" but promote individual stars. Hypocrisy kills change.
Success Formula
Key Success Factor: Align symbols, systems, and rewards with desired culture. Make it safe to behave differently. Be patient. Culture change is a multi-year journey requiring sustained commitment.
