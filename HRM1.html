📖 HRM: DEFINITIONS, NATURE & SCOPE OVERVIEW
Four Core Definitions | Seven Key Characteristics of HRM Nature
HRM
FOUR CORE DEFINITIONS
Edwin Flippo "HRM is the planning, organising, directing and controlling of the procurement, development, compensation, integration, maintenance and separation of human resources"
Gary Dessler "HRM refers to the practices and policies needed to carry out the personnel aspects of a management job"
Dale Yoder "HRM deals with the effective control and use of manpower as distinguished from other sources of power"
Scott, Clothier & Spriegel "HRM is responsible for concentrating on those aspects of operations primarily concerned with the relationship of management to employees"
SEVEN CHARACTERISTICS OF HRM NATURE
Pervasive Function Present in ALL organisations at ALL management levels - CEO to supervisor. Not limited to HR department
Action-Oriented Focuses on SOLUTIONS to HR problems, not theoretical elaborations. Practical and implementable
Future-Oriented Helps org achieve FUTURE GOALS by providing competent, motivated employees. Anticipatory, not reactive
Development-Oriented Develops FULL POTENTIAL of employees through training, work assignments, career planning
Integrating Mechanism Integrates individual GOALS with ORGANISATIONAL OBJECTIVES; builds cordial relations between employees and management
Interdisciplinary Draws from psychology, sociology, economics, law, management theory, industrial relations
Comprehensive Covers ALL decisions, strategies, factors relating to MANAGEMENT OF PEOPLE at all levels
KEY EXAM INSIGHT
Flippo's Definition Most Used Covers 8 activities: planning, organising, directing, controlling, procurement, development, compensation, integration, maintenance, separation. Use this in 20M answers for comprehensive coverage

🔄 SCOPE OF HRM: IIPM CLASSIFICATION & SIX CORE FUNCTIONS
Personnel | Welfare | Industrial Relations Aspects | Six HR Functions Framework
HRM SCOPE
THREE ASPECTS (IIPM)
Personnel Aspect HRP, job analysis, recruitment, selection, placement, induction, transfer, promotion, separation, retrenchment, resignation
Welfare Aspect Working conditions, canteens, creches, housing, education, health, recreation, medical assistance, employee benefits
Industrial Relations Aspect Union-management relations, collective bargaining, grievance handling, disciplinary procedures, strike management, labour laws compliance
SIX HR FUNCTIONS
PROCUREMENT HRP, Job Analysis, Job Description, Job Specification, Recruitment, Selection, Placement, Induction. Goal: Acquire RIGHT people for RIGHT jobs
DEVELOPMENT Training, Management Development, Career Planning, Organisational Development, Skill enhancement. Goal: BUILD capability and competence
COMPENSATION Wage & Salary Administration, Job Evaluation, Pay scales, Incentives, Fringe Benefits, Performance bonuses. Goal: REWARD fairly and competitively
INTEGRATION Motivation, Morale building, Grievance Handling, Trade Union Relations, Collective Bargaining, Industrial Relations. Goal: ALIGN individual & organisational interests
MAINTENANCE Health & Safety, Employee Welfare, Personnel Records, HR Audit, Legal compliance. Goal: SUSTAIN healthy work environment
SEPARATION Retirement, Resignation handling, Retrenchment, VRS (Voluntary Retirement Scheme), Death benefits, Exit Interviews. Goal: MANAGE employee departures professionally

📚 EVOLUTION OF HRM: SIX STAGES (PYQ 2018 - 20M)
Scientific Management >> Digital HRM | F.W. Taylor to AI-Driven HR
6 STAGES
STAGE 1-2: PRE-MODERN ERA
Stage 1: Scientific Management (Pre-1930) F.W. Taylor: Workers as ECONOMIC BEINGS; time-motion study; personnel = labour management; focus on efficiency and output; labour seen as a cost
Stage 2: Human Relations (1930s-50s) Elton Mayo (Hawthorne experiments): Social factors MATTER; informal groups influence performance; recognition of psychological needs; pioneered group dynamics research
STAGE 3-4: MODERN ERA
Stage 3: Behavioural Science (1950s-70s) Maslow, McGregor, Herzberg: Individual NEEDS and motivations central; job design matters; participative management; treating employees as SELF-ACTUALISING beings
Stage 4: HRM Era (1970s-90s) HR as an ASSET, not a cost; integrated with organisational STRATEGY; policies formalised; shift from reactive to proactive; HR department gains legitimacy and power
STAGE 5-6: CONTEMPORARY ERA
Stage 5: Strategic HRM (1990s-2010s) Ulrich's HR Roles: STRATEGIC PARTNER (align HR to business goals) + ADMIN EXPERT (efficient delivery) + EMPLOYEE CHAMPION (advocate for employees) + CHANGE AGENT (drive transformation)
Stage 6: Digital/Agile HRM (2010s-Now) E-HRM systems, HR Analytics, AI in recruitment/screening, gig workforce management, remote work policies, data-driven HR decisions, real-time people analytics, digital employer branding

🔀 PERSONNEL MANAGEMENT vs HRM: KEY DIFFERENCES
Paradigm Shift from Labour Management to Human Resource Development
PM vs HRM
FUNDAMENTAL PHILOSOPHY
Personnel Management View Labour as a COST TO MINIMISE. Workers are production inputs. Focus on cost control and efficiency. View: adversarial relationship with workers. Industrial relations = conflict management
HRM View Humans as ASSETS/RESOURCES TO DEVELOP. Employees are value creators. Focus on capability building and engagement. View: partnership relationship. Industrial relations = mutual benefit
OPERATIONAL APPROACH
Personnel Management: REACTIVE Fire-fighting approach. Responds to crises. Short-term, problem-focused. Collective bargaining as CENTRAL activity. Industrial relations = strike management
HRM: PROACTIVE Anticipatory approach. Prevents problems. Long-term, developmental focus. Individual contracts emphasis. Direct employer-employee RELATIONSHIP. Industrial relations = collaboration
EMPLOYEE RELATIONS STYLE
Personnel Management: CONFORMANCE Emphasis on rules & compliance. "Obey or face consequences". Employee as rule-follower. Top-down control. Little discretion or autonomy
HRM: COMMITMENT Emphasis on values & culture. "Do what's right for org". Employee as value-creator. Empowerment and discretion. Discretionary effort encouraged through engagement

🌍 HRM IS CULTURE-BOUND: HOFSTEDE'S DIMENSIONS (PYQ 2020 - 20M)
How National Culture Shapes HRM Practices | Four Hofstede Dimensions
HOFSTEDE
POWER DISTANCE
HIGH Power Distance (India, China) Top-down appraisal; seniority-based promotions; hierarchical structures; respect for authority; formal communication; power concentrated at top
LOW Power Distance (Sweden, Denmark) 360° feedback; participative appraisals; merit-based promotions; flat hierarchies; informal communication; power distributed; questioning authority acceptable
INDIVIDUALISM vs COLLECTIVISM
HIGH Individualism (USA, Australia) Individual pay based on merit; personal career planning; individual recognition; competition encouraged; loyalty to self first; task-oriented
HIGH Collectivism (Japan, India) Group incentives; seniority-based pay; collective planning; group recognition; cooperation valued; loyalty to group/organisation; relationship-oriented
UNCERTAINTY AVOIDANCE
HIGH Uncertainty Avoidance (Germany, Greece) Detailed Job Descriptions; formal procedures; rules-based HR; risk aversion; structured career paths; clear reporting lines; extensive training before taking responsibilities
LOW Uncertainty Avoidance (UK, USA) Flexible roles; tolerance for ambiguity; outcome-focused evaluation; entrepreneurship encouraged; risk-taking rewarded; informal procedures; adaptability valued
MASCULINITY vs FEMININITY
HIGH Masculinity (Japan, USA) Competitive pay; performance-driven culture; long working hours; career advancement emphasis; achievement-oriented; aggressive competition; work-centredness
HIGH Femininity (Scandinavia, Netherlands) Work-life balance emphasis; maternity/paternity leave; welfare provisions; cooperation valued; quality of life important; modest income differentials; social responsibility

👥 DAVE ULRICH HR ROLES MODEL (1997) & HRP DEFINITIONS
Four HR Roles | Three Core HRP Definitions | Coleman, Stainer, Wickstrom
ULRICH + HRP
FOUR ULRICH HR ROLES
Strategic Partner Aligns HR strategy with business strategy. Participates in strategic planning. Long-term workforce planning. Helps achieve competitive advantage through people
Administrative Expert Efficient delivery of HR processes (payroll, compliance, records). Systems and procedures focus. Cost efficiency. HRIS implementation. Operational excellence
Employee Champion Advocates for employee needs to management. Grievance handling. Engagement surveys. Welfare and support. Represents employee voice. Builds loyalty and commitment
Change Agent Drives and manages organisational change. Cultural transformation. Restructuring support. Change communication. Innovation champion. Manages resistance to change
HRP DEFINITIONS (Part B Foundation)
Coleman Definition "HRP is the process of determining manpower requirements and the means of meeting those requirements in order to carry out the integrated plan of the organisation"
Stainer Definition "Manpower planning is the strategy for the acquisition, utilisation, improvement and preservation of an enterprise's human resources"
Wickstrom Definition HRP = Forecasting future requirements + Inventory of present resources + Identifying deviation + Planning recruitment/training/development to meet future needs
KEY PYQ LINE "HRP is a PREREQUISITE for EFFECTIVE MANAGEMENT of human resources" - Use this in answers to establish importance

📋 HRP PROCESS: 7-STEP MODEL (PYQ 2015 - 20M)
Complete Framework from Analysis to Monitoring | Critical for Exam
7 STEPS
STEPS 1-3: ANALYSIS & FORECASTING
Step 1: Analyse Present Manpower Inventory SKILLS INVENTORY: Record current employees' skills, qualifications, experience, competencies. STRENGTHS & GAPS identification. DATA: Age, tenure, education, department, role, performance. Understand "what we have now"
Step 2: Forecast Manpower Demand (Future Requirement) MACRO FACTORS: Business strategy, market growth, expansion plans, new initiatives. MICRO FACTORS: Retirements, resignations, natural attrition rates. METHODS: Trend analysis, regression, ratio analysis, Delphi technique. QUESTION: "How many people do we need in 2-3 years?"
Step 3: Forecast Manpower Supply (Future Availability) INTERNAL SUPPLY: Promotions, transfers, training outcomes from current workforce. EXTERNAL SUPPLY: Labour market conditions, unemployment rates, education output, competitor hiring. QUESTION: "Where will we get people from?"
STEPS 4-7: MATCHING & ACTION
Step 4: Identify Manpower Gap (Deviation Analysis) GAP = Demand - Supply. If Demand > Supply = SHORTAGE (need recruitment, outsourcing). If Supply > Demand = SURPLUS (need redeployment, retrenchment, VRS, natural attrition). CRITICAL FOR PLANNING
Step 5: Plan HR Actions (Recruitment, Development, Separation) SHORTAGE response: Recruitment plan (internal/external), outsourcing, training acceleration. SURPLUS response: Redeployment to other departments, retraining for different roles, VRS, managed attrition. Coordinate with Finance for budget
Step 6: Implement HR Policies & Programs Execute recruitment drives, training programs, restructuring. Communicate changes. Support affected employees. Manage transitions smoothly
Step 7: Monitor & Review (Ongoing) Track actual vs planned hiring, attrition, development outcomes. Measure HRP effectiveness (fill rates, time-to-hire, retention). Adjust for changing business needs. ANNUAL REVIEW minimum
