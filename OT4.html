Authority & Control
👥 Bases of Authority (Weber)
Three Authority Types
Traditional
Authority based on custom, inheritance, long-standing practice. "This is how it's always done."
Charismatic
Authority from personal magnetism, inspirational qualities. Followers attracted to leader's personality and vision.
Rational-Legal
Authority from formal position and rules. "I have authority because the rules say so." Modern bureaucracies.
Span of Management
Wide Span
Many subordinates per manager. Flat structure, faster decisions, but manager overload risk.
Narrow Span
Few subordinates per manager. Tall structure, close supervision, but slower decisions and costly.
Graicunas Formula
Theory: Manager's workload grows exponentially with subordinates (not linearly). With 5 subordinates = 125 possible relationships. Optimal span: 4-6 people.
Control Levels
Strategic: Board and CEO. Long-term direction and policy
Tactical: Middle management. Implement strategy, manage departments
Operational: Supervisors and teams. Day-to-day tasks and delivery

Line & Staff Functions
⚙️ Authority Types
Line Functions (Primary)
Command Authority: Direct authority over subordinates
Direct Goal Achievement: Directly contribute to organization's primary mission
Accountability: Held responsible for results. Operations, Sales, Production
Staff Functions (Support)
Advisory Authority: Recommend, not command. HR advises but doesn't hire everyone
Specialist Expertise: Provide specialized knowledge. Finance, Legal, IT
Service Role: Support line functions to be more effective
Conflict Causes
Different Orientations: Line focuses on short-term results; Staff focuses on long-term best practices. Line is action-oriented; Staff is analytical.
Authority Creep: Staff tries to increase authority; Line resents "interference" in operations. Status anxiety on both sides.
Information Asymmetry: Staff often has better information; Line feels uninformed. Blame dynamics when things go wrong.
Modern Evolution
Business Partner Model
HR embeds in business units, collaborates with line, still maintains standards. Reduces line-staff conflict.
Shared Service Centers
Staff functions centralized, serve all business units as internal service providers. Efficiency and standardization.

Structure Types
🏗️ Organisational Structures
Functional Structure
Features
Grouped by function (HR, Finance, Ops, Marketing). Deep expertise, economies of scale. Problem: Silo mentality, slow cross-functional work.
Matrix Structure
Features
Dual authority (functional + project). Complex but flexible. Problem: Violates unity of command, confusion over who's in charge, political friction.
Project-Based Structure
Features
Organized around projects/deliverables. High goal clarity, temporary teams. Problem: Resource duplication, team instability, less focus on individual development.
Power & Politics
Reward Power
Ability to give bonuses, promotions, benefits. "I can reward you."
Coercive Power
Ability to punish, fire, demote. "I can hurt you."
Legitimate
Authority from position. "I have formal authority."
Expert Power
From knowledge/skills. "I know." Creates dependency.
Referent Power
From likability/respect. "People like me." Charisma.
Information Control
From gatekeeping data. "I have information."

Politics & IT Impact
🎭 Political Dynamics
Sources of Organisational Politics
Resource Scarcity: Competition for budgets, staff, equipment drives political behavior
Ambiguity of Goals: When goals unclear, people pursue own interpretations
Differential Information: Some have more/better information than others
Political Tactics
Coalition Building: Ally with others to accumulate power
Control of Information: Gate-keep critical data
Visibility Seeking: Ensure own contributions are noticed
Strategic Positioning: Move to roles with more power
Ethics in Power & Politics
Power Corruption: "Power tends to corrupt; absolute power corrupts absolutely." Need checks and accountability mechanisms.
Transparency Needs: Open communication, clear criteria, fair processes reduce political gaming and increase trust.
IT Impact on Structure & Control
Flatter Hierarchies: Direct communication bypasses middle management
Decentralisation: Remote work enables distributed decision-making
Algorithmic Management: AI and data drive decisions, reducing political influence
Modern Organisational Forms
Virtual Organisations
Teams work remotely, coordinated through technology. Flexibility but isolation.
Fishnet Metaphor
Interconnected nodes with no center. Network organizations. Emergent properties.
Digital Paradox
Contradiction: IT enables both surveillance (digital panopticon, total control) and autonomy (remote work, flexibility). Organizations choose based on philosophy and trust level.
