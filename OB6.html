⚡ CONFLICT: CONCEPT & EVOLUTION OF VIEWS
Definitions, Types, and Perspectives (EVERY YEAR TOPIC - PYQ 2014-2023)
CONFLICT
DEFINITIONS
Robbins (Most Used) "Process that begins when one party perceives another party has negatively affected something the first party cares about"
Core Element Perceived incompatibility of goals, values, or actions between interdependent parties
TYPES OF CONFLICT
Intrapersonal Within individual; psychological (Approach-Approach, Avoidance-Avoidance, Approach-Avoidance) - PYQ 2022 (10M)
Interpersonal Between individuals; personality, values, resource competition
Intragroup Within team; Task Conflict (functional at moderate levels) vs Relationship Conflict (always dysfunctional)
Intergroup Between departments; Sales vs Production; HR vs Finance
Interorganisational Between organisations; competitive, regulatory, supply chain
3 VIEWS ON CONFLICT
Traditional (Pre-1940s) Conflict always dysfunctional; to be avoided at all costs
Human Relations (1940s-1970s) Conflict is natural; acceptance of low-moderate conflict as inevitable
INTERACTIONIST (1970s-Present) ⭐⭐ DOMINANT VIEW - Some conflict is FUNCTIONAL; optimal level exists. Too little = groupthink, complacency, stagnation. Too much = chaos, breakdown. Managers must maintain productive middle ground

📊 PONDY'S 5-STAGE CONFLICT PROCESS MODEL (1967)
Mnemonic: Lazy People Feel More Annoyed = L-P-F-M-A
PONDY MODEL
5 STAGES
Stage 1: LATENT Conditions creating potential: resource scarcity, goal divergence, role ambiguity, interdependence, communication barriers
Stage 2: PERCEIVED One party becomes aware of conflict conditions; cognitive recognition of incompatibility
Stage 3: FELT Emotional tension, anxiety, hostility; felt psychologically by parties
Stage 4: MANIFEST Visible through behaviour: open aggression, passive resistance, grievances, work slowdowns
Stage 5: AFTERMATH Outcomes of resolved/unresolved conflict; foundation for future conflicts or cooperation
INTERVENTION POINT
BEST: Latent or Perceived Stages Before emotional escalation; easier to resolve; prevents manifesting into open conflict and aftermath damage

🎯 THOMAS-KILMANN 5 CONFLICT-HANDLING STYLES (PYQ 2021)
Mnemonic: Can Crows Catch Any Ant? = Competing, Collaborating, Compromising, Accommodating, Avoiding
5 STYLES
COMPETING
(High Assertiveness, Low Cooperation) Win-lose; pushy; power-oriented; goal-focused
When to Use Quick decisions needed; principles at stake; emergencies; unpopular decisions must be made
Limitation Damages long-term relationships; creates resentment; breeds future conflict
COLLABORATING
(High A, High C) - IDEAL for Complex Issues Win-win; problem-solving; integration
When to Use Complex issues; both parties' concerns matter; long-term relationship important; innovation needed
Limitation Time-consuming; requires trust and skill; not suitable for crisis or time-pressure
COMPROMISING
(Medium A, Medium C) - Middle Ground Give-and-take; splitting difference
When to Use Time pressure; equal power parties; temporary solution needed; all-or-nothing impractical (Labour negotiations)
Limitation Neither party fully satisfied; 'lose-lose' feeling; may not address underlying causes
ACCOMMODATING & AVOIDING
ACCOMMODATING (Low A, High C) Yield; harmony-preserving. When: issue minor for you; opponent clearly right; building goodwill
AVOIDING (Low A, Low C) Withdraw; postpone. When: cooling-off time needed; issue trivial; cost > benefit; need more info. Limitation: unresolved; may fester

✅ FUNCTIONAL CONFLICT & ROLE CONFLICT (PYQ 2019, 2014)
Benefits of Conflict | Role Conflict Types & Causes
FUNCTIONAL & ROLE
FUNCTIONAL CONFLICT BENEFITS
Stimulates Creativity Disagreement forces parties beyond assumptions; competing ideas generate better solutions (Jehn, 1995)
Prevents Groupthink Moderate conflict ensures critical evaluation (Janis, 1972 - NASA Challenger, Enron examples)
Surfaces Hidden Problems Poor processes, inequitable treatment, role ambiguity brought to light through conflict
Drives Organisational Change Pressure for adaptive response; organisations stagnate without internal challenge
Improves Decision Quality Multiple perspectives considered; reduces cognitive bias (FAE reduction)
Strengthens Relationships Constructively resolved conflict builds mutual respect and understanding
ROLE CONFLICT TYPES
Inter-role Conflict Between two roles (work vs family - classic work-life balance)
Intra-role Conflict Within same role, conflicting expectations from different stakeholders (speed vs quality)
Person-Role Conflict Role requirements vs personal values/ethics (asked to misrepresent data)
Role Overload Too many expectations for available time/resources; quantitative overload; stress and poor performance
Role Ambiguity Unclear role definition; uncertain expectations; anxiety; poor performance (Pondy Stage 1 cause)
ROLE CONFLICT CAUSES
Structural Matrix/project structures with multiple reporting lines (fundamental cause)
Ambiguous Job Descriptions Overlapping jurisdictions; unclear expectations
Organisational Change Restructuring without role redefinition; rapid change creating uncertainty
Diverse Values & Culture Different expectations across cultural groups; diversity without inclusive management

🔧 CONFLICT RESOLUTION STRATEGIES & CURRENT AFFAIRS
Strategies from Pondy's Aftermath | India Context
RESOLUTION
RESOLUTION STRATEGIES
Dominance/Forcing Use authority/power to end conflict; imposed solution (aligns with Competing style)
Compromise/Negotiation Both parties give up something; split difference; middle-ground solution
Smoothing/Accommodation De-emphasise differences; focus on common ground; preserve harmony (aligns with Accommodating)
Confrontation/Problem-Solving Address root cause; seek integrative solutions (aligns with Collaborating); win-win orientation
Withdrawal/Avoidance Retreat from conflict; postpone decision; buy time (aligns with Avoiding)
JOHARI WINDOW & CONFLICT
OPEN AREA (Arena) Known to both parties - shared understanding reduces conflict
BLIND AREA Unknown to self, known to others - my blind spots cause conflict with others (unaware of impact)
HIDDEN AREA Known to self, hidden from others - concealed resentments fester; unexpressed needs breed conflict
Conflict Prevention Enlarge Open Area through feedback (shrink Blind) and disclosure (shrink Hidden)
CURRENT AFFAIRS 2024-25
Industrial Disputes Semiconductor industry conflicts; IT sector labour disputes; Gig worker organising
POSH Act 2013 Enforcement Workplace sexual harassment - interpersonal conflict legislation; ICC requirements
Industrial Relations Code 2020 Formalised mechanisms for conflict resolution in organisations
Diversity & Inclusion Challenges Inter-group conflicts along caste, gender, regional lines; DEI initiatives attempt prevention

📈 TASK vs RELATIONSHIP CONFLICT - CRITICAL DISTINCTION
Jehn (1995) Research | Interactionist View Application
TASK vs RELATION
TASK CONFLICT (Cognitive)
Nature Disagreement about content, goals, methods of work; intellectual disagreement
At MODERATE Levels: FUNCTIONAL Stimulates critical thinking; prevents groupthink; improves decision quality (Jehn research)
At HIGH Levels: DYSFUNCTIONAL Becomes coordination breakdown; decision paralysis; implementation failure
Management Goal Maintain MODERATE levels; direct toward constructive outcomes; decouple from relationship conflict
RELATIONSHIP CONFLICT (Affective)
Nature Disagreement about interpersonal issues; personality clashes; trust/respect issues
At ANY Level: DYSFUNCTIONAL Even low levels damage team cohesion, communication, psychological safety
Effect on Performance Reduces cooperation, increases absenteeism, kills innovation; spreads toxic culture
Management Goal Minimize or eliminate relationship conflict; build trust and psychological safety
KEY INSIGHT FOR INTERACTIONIST VIEW
Optimal Conflict = Task Only, At Moderate Levels Managers must promote task debate while protecting relationships
Prevention of Relationship Conflict Escalation Address task conflict early before it becomes personal; maintain psychological safety during debates
