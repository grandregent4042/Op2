📚 OB: MEANING, DEFINITIONS & S-O-B-C MODEL
Core Definitions & Conceptual Framework
OB BASICS
CORE DEFINITIONS
Luthans (2011) "OB is directly concerned with understanding, predicting, and controlling human behaviour in organisations"
Robbins "OB investigates impact of individuals, groups, structure on behaviour within organisations for improving effectiveness"
Davis "Study and application of knowledge about how people act within organisations - human tool for human benefit"
Key Point QUOTE DIRECTLY - Definitions are frequently asked in exams; memorise at least 2-3
S-O-B-C MODEL (PYQ 2015)
S - Stimulus External environmental event triggering response (perf appraisal, new policy, supervisor instruction)
O - Organism Individual with personality, attitudes, perceptions mediating stimulus and behaviour (Big Five traits, mood)
B - Behaviour Observable actions resulting from stimulus filtered through organism (effort, innovation, resignation)
C - Consequence Outcomes following behaviour; become stimuli for future behaviour through reinforcement (promotion, praise)
ENRICHING S-O-B-C (PYQ 2015)
Cognitive Mediators Add values, emotions, biases to O component - not just traits
Social Context Include informal group norms (Hawthorne studies) affecting behaviour
Org Context Structure, culture, reward systems shape behaviour beyond individual factors
Feedback Loops Consequences become new stimuli - cyclical process, not linear
Individual Differences PERSONALITY THEORY is key enrichment - critical for OB understanding
WHY INDIVIDUAL DIFFERENCES
Personality Determines habitual processing, response to challenge, interpersonal style - shapes job performance
Perception Explains why same event creates different responses in different people
Motivation Force activating, directing, sustaining behaviour - explains performance variation among equals
Attitudes Job satisfaction, commitment predict absenteeism, turnover, citizenship behaviour
Values Fundamental beliefs shaping ethical behaviour, career choices, response to demands
EXAM TIP: S-O-B-C + ENRICHMENT
Remember: Basic S-O-B-C is stimulus-organism-behaviour-consequence linear model
PYQ 2015: Be ready to ENRICH it with 5 dimensions beyond basic framework

👤 PERSONALITY: DEFINITIONS & CHARACTERISTICS
What is Personality & Its Key Features
PERSONALITY
CORE DEFINITIONS
Allport (1937) "Dynamic organisation within individual of psychophysical systems determining unique adjustments to environment" - Classic; emphasises uniqueness & dynamic nature
Robbins "Sum total of ways individual reacts to and interacts with others" - Concise; use for 10M answers
Cattell "That which permits prediction of what person will do in given situation" - Predictive dimension linking personality to performance
Functional Personality = Nature (heredity) + Nurture (environment) - Introduces nature-nurture debate
6 KEY CHARACTERISTICS
Stable & Enduring Relatively stable allowing prediction; basis for using personality in selection
Unique No two personalities identical - individual differences the focus
Consistent Consistent across situations though situational factors moderate expression
Nature & Nurture Influenced by both - ~50% heritability estimate for Big Five traits
Measurable Validated instruments available (MBTI, Big Five, NEO-PI-R, 16PF)
Not Fixed Can change slowly with significant life experiences - not immutable
MEASUREMENT & VALIDITY
Validated Instruments MBTI, Big Five, NEO-PI-R, 16PF questionnaires available for assessment
Predictive Validity Personality predicts job performance, leadership, team fit, turnover
OB Application Selection: hire for trait-role fit; Development: leverage strengths; Placement: role-personality alignment
NATURE VS NURTURE
Heritability Evidence Twin studies (Minnesota Registry): MZ twins apart show ~50% concordance
Big Five Heritability 40-60% heritability - roughly half personality is genetic
OB Implication Selection may be more effective than training for some personality dimensions
DEFINITION STRATEGY FOR EXAM
Use Allport for 20M: Emphasises dynamic, unique, psychophysical - comprehensive
Use Robbins for 10M: Concise sum-total definition fits word limit
Use Cattell for predictive context: When discussing performance management

🧬 DETERMINANTS OF PERSONALITY: HEREDITY & ENVIRONMENT
Nature vs Nurture Debate (PYQ 2025, 2021)
DETERMINANTS
HEREDITY / BIOLOGICAL
Genetic Foundation Molecular structure of genes in chromosomes - personality partly 'hard-wired' from birth
Twin Studies Evidence Minnesota Twin Registry: MZ twins raised apart show ~50% personality concordance - demonstrates genetic contribution independent of environment
Big Five Heritability 40-60% heritability for Big Five traits - roughly half of personality variation is genetic
Eysenck's Theory Linked extraversion to arousal threshold of reticular activating system - biological basis of personality
OB Implication Selection (hiring for fit) may be more effective than training in some personality dimensions
ENVIRONMENT / CULTURAL
Cultural Context Collectivist cultures (India, Japan) produce group-oriented personalities; individualist (US, UK) produce autonomous personalities (Hofstede)
Family Influences Parenting styles (authoritative, authoritarian, permissive) predict personality outcomes - primary socialisation embeds core values
Social Learning (Bandura) Personality shaped by observing and modelling significant others - we learn who to be by watching important people
Peer Group Influence Especially in adolescence, peers shape emerging personality through norms and identity structures
Situational Strength (Mischel) Strong situations suppress personality; weak situations allow personality to manifest freely
NATURE-NURTURE INTERACTION
Both Operate Together Personality is product of BOTH heredity AND environment - not either/or but interaction
Rough 50-50 Split Approximately 50% genetic, 50% environmental for most Big Five traits
Gene-Environment Interaction Genotype predisposes but environment actualises - genes set potential, environment determines actualisation
Timing Effects Early environmental experiences (first 5 years) have disproportionate impact on personality formation
PYQ CONTEXT - DETERMINANTS COVERAGE
PYQ 2025 (10M): Heredity, environment, situation; twin studies; Mischel's situational strength
PYQ 2021 (20M): Determinants of personality + major attributes - full coverage
Answer Strategy: Balance nature AND nurture; cite twin study evidence; discuss situational moderation

🎭 PERSONALITY THEORIES: FREUD & ERIKSON DEVELOPMENTAL
Psychoanalytic & Psychosocial Approaches (PYQ 2019, 2017)
THEORIES
FREUD'S ID-EGO-SUPEREGO
ID (Pleasure Principle) Primitive, unconscious, pleasure-seeking - seeks immediate gratification of biological drives without reality consideration
EGO (Reality Principle) Rational, conscious mediator - delays gratification, finds socially acceptable ways to satisfy ID impulses
SUPEREGO (Moral Conscience) Moral component - conscience punishes violations with guilt; ego-ideal rewards compliance with pride. Internalised social standards
Dynamic Tension Personality = ongoing ID-SUPEREGO conflict mediated by EGO. Anxiety triggers defence mechanisms (repression, projection, rationalisation)
FREUD'S PSYCHOSEXUAL STAGES
Oral (0-18m) Weaning; trust vs mistrust fixation leads to dependency OR aggression; oral regression (smoking, overeating)
Anal (18m-3y) Toilet training; anal retentive = orderliness, rigidity, miserliness (bureaucratic); anal expulsive = messiness, rebelliousness
Phallic (3-6y) Genitals; Oedipus/Electra complex; fixation leads to authority difficulties, narcissism, recklessness
Latency (6-12y) Sexual impulses repressed; energy to intellectual/social skills - no fixation if earlier resolved
Genital (Puberty+) Mature sexuality toward peers - capacity for love, work, social cooperation; mature personality outcome
ERIKSON'S 8 PSYCHOSOCIAL STAGES
Trust vs Mistrust (0-18m) Can I trust world? >> HOPE virtue. Negative: fear, anxiety
Autonomy vs Shame (18m-3y) Can I do things myself? >> WILL virtue. Negative: shame, doubt
Initiative vs Guilt (3-6y) Am I allowed to be myself? >> PURPOSE virtue. Negative: guilt, inhibition
Industry vs Inferiority (6-12y) Can I make it? >> COMPETENCE virtue. Negative: inferiority, inadequacy
Identity vs Confusion (Teen) Who am I? >> FIDELITY virtue. Negative: identity diffusion, role confusion
Intimacy vs Isolation, Generativity vs Stagnation, Integrity vs Despair Adult stages - young adult, middle, old age. OB focus on adolescent onwards for personality formation
CRITICAL EVALUATION
Freud STRENGTHS Introduced unconscious, defence mechanisms, developmental perspective - foundational to psychology
Freud WEAKNESSES Unfalsifiable claims, androcentric bias, overemphasis on sexuality, small non-representative samples
Modern Relevance Attachment theory, defence mechanisms, implicit bias research trace to Freud. OB: unconscious motivations in workplace
Erikson Advantage Lifespan perspective, less sex-focused, culturally broader, validated more robustly than Freud
PYQ PREPARATION - DEVELOPMENTAL THEORIES
PYQ 2019 (10M): Critically examine Freud's psychosexual stages - 5 stages + evaluation required
PYQ 2017 (10M): Freud + biological factors - agree with qualifications
Strategy: Know all stages; emphasise fixation outcomes; critical evaluation is essential

⭐ THE BIG FIVE (OCEAN) PERSONALITY TRAITS
Highest Priority - PYQ 2024 (10M) Direct Question
BIG FIVE
CRITICAL: PYQ 2024
Direct Question (10M) "What are Big Five traits? Which has biggest impact on performance? How would knowledge help manager?"
ANSWER: CONSCIENTIOUSNESS Biggest impact on performance - Barrick & Mount (1991) meta-analysis of 162 studies across ALL occupations
Predictive Validity r=0.23 - strongest personality predictor of job performance universally
Manager Application Use in hiring - conscientiousness predicts performance better than other traits. Training less effective than selection for this trait
O: OPENNESS TO EXPERIENCE
What It Measures Curiosity, creativity, imagination, receptiveness to new ideas and experiences
High Scorer Profile Creative, unconventional, intellectually curious, open to change, flexible thinker
OB Research Finding Predicts performance in creative/R&D roles; adapts well to organisational change; AI upskilling adaptability
Role Fit Best for innovation, strategy, research, change management roles
C: CONSCIENTIOUSNESS ⭐⭐⭐
What It Measures Organisation, dependability, goal-directedness, self-discipline, thoroughness
High Scorer Profile Organised, reliable, diligent, punctual, persistent, detail-oriented, disciplined
STRONGEST PERFORMANCE PREDICTOR Barrick & Mount (1991) - most powerful personality predictor across ALL job types and organisations
Additional Outcomes Predicts academic performance, longevity, health behaviour, career success, financial stability
Selection Priority Should be TOP priority in hiring across all roles - universal predictor
E: EXTRAVERSION
What It Measures Sociability, assertiveness, positive emotionality, dominance, talkativeness
High Scorer Profile Outgoing, assertive, talkative, enthusiastic, sociable, leader-like, energetic
KEY FINDING: Leadership EMERGENCE Predicts who emerges as leader (not necessarily effectiveness) - associates lead more often
Role Fit Suits sales, leadership, training, client-facing roles. Better networkers, better at relationship building
A: AGREEABLENESS
What It Measures Cooperation, trust, empathy, helpfulness, altruism, modesty, tender-mindedness
High Scorer Profile Warm, cooperative, conflict-averse, empathetic, trusting, helpful, considerate
OB Research Finding Predicts teamwork and organisational citizenship behaviour (OCB) - helping colleagues beyond role requirements
Dark Side Too high = difficulty with hard decisions, poor salary negotiation, conflict avoidance problems. Low agreeableness better for negotiations
N: NEUROTICISM (⬇️ desired)
What It Measures Emotional instability, anxiety, moodiness, tenseness, worry, irritability
High Scorer Profile Anxious, moody, easily upset, volatile, insecure, hypersensitive to criticism
NEGATIVE PERFORMANCE PREDICTOR High N = negative predictor of performance, satisfaction, leadership. Low N (Emotional Stability) = resilience under pressure
Role Fit Low neuroticism essential for high-stress roles (crisis, ER, armed forces, leadership)
BIG FIVE MANAGER APPLICATION GUIDE
All hiring: Conscientiousness > all other traits
Sales/Leadership: Extraversion (emergence) + Conscientiousness
High-stress roles: Low Neuroticism (Emotional Stability) crucial
Teamwork: Agreeableness for cooperation; Conscientiousness for follow-through
Innovation/Change: Openness + Conscientiousness combination ideal

🎯 PERSONALITY THEORIES: TYPES, TRAITS, HUMANISTIC & SOCIAL LEARNING
Historical Frameworks to Modern Approaches
THEORIES
TYPE THEORIES
Hippocrates Humours (400 BCE) Sanguine (optimistic, sociable) | Choleric (aggressive, quick-tempered) | Melancholic (analytical, depressive) | Phlegmatic (calm, unemotional) - First systematic classification
Type A vs Type B (Friedman & Rosenman) Type A: competitive, impatient, time-urgent, workaholic, hostile - high CHD risk. Type B: relaxed, less competitive. OB: Type A in pressure roles but burnout risk
Introversion vs Extraversion (Jung) Introvert energised by solitude; Extravert energised by social interaction - Foundation of MBTI and Big Five Extraversion
TRAIT THEORIES
Allport's Trait Theory 3 levels: Cardinal traits (single dominant, like Machiavellian) | Central traits (5-10 characteristics) | Secondary traits (situational, inconsistent) - First systematic trait theory
Cattell's 16PF Factor analysis of 4,500 trait names reduced to 16 source traits. 16PF questionnaire still used in corporate assessment - more granular than Big Five
Eysenck's PEN Model Psychoticism + Extraversion + Neuroticism. Each dimension linked to nervous system functioning - biological basis of personality
HUMANISTIC THEORIES
Maslow - Self-Actualisation Highest-level drive - become what one is capable of. Self-actualising employees seek challenging work, autonomy, growth - cannot motivate purely by salary
Carl Rogers - Person-Centred Self-concept core of personality. Healthy personality when actual self and ideal self are CONGRUENT. Incongruence produces anxiety. Managers' unconditional positive regard supports personality health
Third Force in Psychology Alternative to behavioural and psychoanalytic - emphasises growth, actualisation, authenticity, self-concept
SOCIAL LEARNING - BANDURA
Observational Learning Personality shaped by observing and modelling significant others - vicarious learning mechanism
SELF-EFFICACY (CRITICAL) "Belief in one's capabilities to organise and execute required courses of action" - MOST consistent individual performance predictor
4 Sources of Self-Efficacy (1) Mastery experiences (most powerful); (2) Vicarious experiences; (3) Verbal persuasion; (4) Physiological states
Reciprocal Determinism Behaviour, person, environment mutually influence each other - no unidirectional causation. Dynamic interaction
THEORY COMPARISON FOR EXAM
Type Theories: Historical, categorical, less validated - mention for completeness
Trait Theories: Scientific, measurable, validated - focus on Big Five
Humanistic: Growth-oriented, applicable to motivation and job design
Social Learning: SELF-EFFICACY is most important - predicts performance better than personality traits

🔑 KEY PERSONALITY ATTRIBUTES: MBTI, LOCUS OF CONTROL & MORE
Specific Personality Measures for Organisational Application
ATTRIBUTES
MBTI - LIMITATIONS & REALITY
4 Dichotomies (E-I, S-N, T-F, J-P) Creates 16 type combinations - widely used in organisations for team building, coaching
⚠️ CRITICAL LIMITATION #1 50% of people get DIFFERENT type on retest after 5 weeks - poor test-retest reliability (unacceptable psychometrically)
⚠️ CRITICAL LIMITATION #2 Limited predictive validity for job performance - does not reliably predict who succeeds in roles
⚠️ CRITICAL LIMITATION #3 False dichotomies - traits are CONTINUOUS spectra, not binary categories (E-I is continuum, not either/or)
BETTER ALTERNATIVE: Big Five Far superior psychometric properties, predicts job performance reliably. MBTI is cultural phenomenon, not validated performance predictor
LOCUS OF CONTROL (ROTTER)
Internal Locus Believe own actions determine outcomes - higher performance in complex roles, lower absenteeism, better suited for leadership and entrepreneurship
External Locus Believe luck/fate controls outcomes - more comfortable in structured, routine, clear-instruction roles with limited responsibility
OB Application Select internals for autonomous roles; externals for structured roles. Consider in hiring and role placement
MACHIAVELLIANISM (MACH IV)
High Mach Profile Pragmatic, emotionally detached, manipulative, believe ends justify means, use people instrumentally
Where They Thrive Situations with direct interaction, loose rules, where emotions irrelevant (negotiations, competitive sales, politics)
OB Concern High Mach risk for unethical behaviour, manipulation, political behaviour - requires ethical guardrails
OTHER KEY ATTRIBUTES
Self-Monitoring High: adapt behaviour to situational cues - flexible, politically astute, good at reading social situations. Low: consistent, authentic "what you see is what you get"
Risk Propensity High: faster decisions with less info - entrepreneurial, dynamic roles. Low: deliberate, thorough - financial control, safety-critical roles
Self-Esteem High SE: job satisfied, less affected by external evaluation, willing to take risks. Low SE: seek approval, less decisive, susceptible to external influence
MBTI vs BIG FIVE FOR EXAM
Exam Strategy: If MBTI question comes, immediately flag limitations and suggest Big Five as better alternative
Remember 3 key limitations: Poor retest reliability (50% different type), no predictive validity for performance, false dichotomies
Use MBTI examples to discuss: Why popular in HR despite poor validation - cultural appeal vs scientific rigour
